# Braindump Prompts

> Cascade uses these questions in PRODUCT_SENSE mode.
> Don't use all of them. Pick the 3–5 that make the user most uncomfortable — those are the important ones.

---

## Before Writing a PRD
- Who is this really for? Not "users" — name a specific person.
- What job are they hiring this product to do?
- What am I assuming about how they'll use this?
- What could go catastrophically wrong?
- Why this? Why now? Why us?
- What would make us 10x more confident before building?

## Before Prioritizing Features
- What evidence do I have that this actually matters to users?
- What's the opportunity cost of doing this instead of [X]?
- Am I in love with the solution instead of the problem?
- If I had to cut this, what would break for users? For the business?
- Who loses if we build this? (There's always someone.)

## Before a Strategy Session
- What's actually working vs. not working? (Be brutally honest.)
- What are we deluding ourselves about?
- What is the market doing that we're not ready for?
- What would make our current strategy completely wrong?
- If a smart new PM joined tomorrow, what would they question first?

## Before Stakeholder Communication
- What does this person actually care about? (Not what they say — what drives them.)
- What's the worst way they could receive this message?
- Am I communicating what I want to say, or what they need to hear?
- What am I leaving out that they'd want to know?
- What objection will they definitely raise, and how do I address it?

## When Stuck on a Decision
- What am I actually afraid of?
- Is this decision reversible? (If yes, just decide and learn.)
- What's the ONE thing that, if I knew it, would make this easy?
- What would I tell a friend to do in this situation?
- What decision would I be comfortable explaining in a year?

## The 30-Second Pre-Flight (Before ANY Major Decision)
1. Can I articulate WHY in one sentence?
2. Have I asked "and then what?" at least twice?
3. Do I know who loses from this?
4. Is there an assumption here I haven't tested?
5. Am I deciding because it's right, or because it's comfortable?

## Braindump Sufficiency Checklist
Before moving to EXECUTION_MODE, confirm:
- [ ] Key assumptions have been named (not just the desired outcome)
- [ ] "What I know" is separated from "what I'm guessing"
- [ ] At least one uncomfortable risk or second-order effect is on the table
- [ ] Something in the braindump challenges the current plan
