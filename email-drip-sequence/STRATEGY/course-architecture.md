# Course Architecture

> How to define your course goal, choose a depth strategy, and build an outline before writing a single email.

---

## Start With the Goal

Before you write anything, define what the reader should be able to **DO** after finishing this course.

Not "understand" — DO. A behavioral outcome, not a knowledge outcome.

| Weak goal | Strong goal |
|-----------|------------|
| Understand investing basics | Open a brokerage account and make their first index fund purchase |
| Learn about email courses | Publish a 10-email course outline and draft their first deep dive email |
| Know about local SEO | Build and submit a local service page with proper JSON-LD schema |

**Test:** Can you write a CTA at the end of the course that directly connects to this outcome? If yes, the goal is concrete enough.

---

## Choose a Monetization Model First

The model determines the tone, depth, and CTA — even though the content is mostly the same either way.

| Model | Price | Depth | CTA |
|-------|-------|-------|-----|
| **Demand Gen** | Free or $5–$20 | Show them everything it takes to self-serve; make them realize the scope | "Here's what working with me looks like..." |
| **Standalone** | $5–$230 | Equip them to get the outcome independently | "Here's the next level if you want to go deeper..." |

**Key insight:** Show people how much work the right outcome requires. When they realize the scope, most will choose to hire rather than DIY. The EBC creates informed buyers who feel good about paying.

---

## Choose a Depth Strategy

| Approach | Description | Best For |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Horizontal** | Cover key lessons across 4-6 areas, no deep dives | Overview/intro courses; multiple upsell targets |
| **Vertical** | Go deep on one area, tease the others | Courses that feed a single flagship service |
| **101** | Cover only what's needed before going deeper | Bottom of a value ladder with courses/services above |

**Rule:** Pick one and commit. Mixed strategies produce courses that feel unfocused.

## Build the Outline Using 7 Questions

Answer these to generate a complete outline. It takes multiple passes — that's normal. Iterate the outline rather than rewriting fully drafted emails.

```
1. What am I talking about?
   → Topic name and one-sentence description

2. Why does it matter and how does it benefit people?
   → The case for why this is worth their time

3. What do they need to know before we dive in?
   → Prerequisites, definitions, framing

4. What are the primary "pillars" of the overall topic?
   → 3-6 major areas (these become chapters or sections)

5. What are the subtopics of those pillars?
   → 2-4 subtopics per pillar (these become deep dive emails)

6. In each subtopic: what are the considerations, challenges,
   objectives, and best practices?
   → The actual content for each email

7. How do they take the knowledge and put it into action?
   → Action items: one per major section
```

## Map Your Email Sequence

Once you have the outline, map it to email types. This becomes your production plan.

### Short Course Template (< 15 emails)

```
Email 1:  Welcome (plain text)
Email 2:  [Section 1] Deep Dive
Email 3:  [Section 2] Deep Dive
Email 4:  [Section 3] Deep Dive
Email 5:  Action Item
Email 6:  [Section 4] Deep Dive
Email 7:  [Section 5] Deep Dive
Email 8:  Summary + CTA (upsell or close)
```

### Full Course Template (≥ 15 emails)

```
Email 1:   Welcome (plain text)
Email 2:   Chapter 1 Overview

[Chapter 1 — 3-4 deep dives]
Email 3:   [1.1] Deep Dive
Email 4:   [1.2] Deep Dive
Email 5:   [1.3] Quiz
Email 6:   [1.4] Action Item

Email 7:   Chapter 2 Overview

[Chapter 2 — 3-4 deep dives]
Email 8:   [2.1] Deep Dive
Email 9:   [2.2] Deep Dive
Email 10:  [2.3] Deep Dive
Email 11:  [2.4] Action Item

Email 12:  Mid-course Feedback Ask

[Chapter 3...]

Email N-2: Chapter N Overview
Email N-1: [Final] Deep Dive or Action Item
Email N:   CTA / Close / Upsell
```

## Subject Line System

Consistent subject lines help readers navigate and find past emails.

| Course Type | Format | Example |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| Multi-chapter | `[#.#] Subject` | `[2.1] Why your vesting schedule matters` |
| Single-chapter | `[Lesson #] Subject` | `[Lesson 3] The hook that stops the scroll` |
| Special emails | Descriptive | `Quick favor — 2 minutes` |

## Pacing and Delivery Rules

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Delivery frequency | 1 email/day during the week |
| Weekend sends | Skip Saturday and Sunday (automate the skip) |
| Self-pacing | Include Fast Forward links in every email |
| Email time | Morning sends outperform (6–8am recipient local time) |

**Fast Forward:** A link inside each email that, when clicked, immediately sends the next email. Lets motivated readers go faster. Essential for evergreen courses.

---

## The 80/20 of EBC Production

| Work Type | % of outcome | Priority |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Getting the outline right | 40% | First |
| Writing the body copy | 40% | Second |
| Building automations | 10% | Third |
| Design and visual polish | 10% | Last |

**Rule:** Build the outline before writing. Write before designing. Design before automating. Don't let production sequence work against you.

---

## Common Outline Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Starting with chapter 1 before the goal is clear | Define the end CTA first, then work backwards |
| Covering too many topics | Pick Horizontal, Vertical, or 101 — not all three |
| Going straight to drafting without iterating the outline | Do 2 full passes on the outline before writing email 1 |
| Writing < 5 emails and calling it a course | Minimum viable course = 8 emails |
