# Sales Call Script

## The Frame

You are not selling. You are diagnosing. If the math works for the prospect, you present the offer. If it doesn't, you say so and move on.

**The goal of the call:** Determine if you can help them. If yes, make them an offer. If no, refer or disqualify.

---

## Pre-Call Checklist

- [ ] Research their LinkedIn (last 3–5 posts)
- [ ] Know their company size and revenue stage
- [ ] Identify their current acquisition method (ads? referrals? cold outreach?)
- [ ] Have the ROI calculator ready (open in a tab)
- [ ] Know your offer cold — price, deliverables, timeline, guarantee

---

## Call Structure (30–45 minutes)

```
1. Frame Setting      (2 min)
2. Discovery          (10–15 min)
3. Pain Amplification (5 min)
4. ROI Math           (5 min)
5. Offer Presentation (5 min)
6. Handle Objections  (5–10 min)
7. Close / Next Steps (2–3 min)
```

---

## 1. Frame Setting (2 min)

Establish the agenda and get permission to ask questions.

```
"Thanks for taking the time. Here's what I want to do — 
I'll ask you a few questions about what's going on with your 
acquisition right now, and based on what you tell me, I'll 
either share how we work or I'll tell you honestly that 
we're probably not the right fit.

Sound fair?"
```

**Why it works:** You remove sales pressure. They relax. They answer honestly.

---

## 2. Discovery (10–15 min)

Understand their current situation before presenting anything.

**Core discovery questions:**
```
"How are you currently getting new clients?"

"What's working about that? What's not?"

"What does a new client typically mean to your business — 
 what's the average contract value?"

"How many new clients are you closing per month right now?"

"Where do you want to be in 6 months?"

"What's the main thing stopping you from getting there?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Their current CAC (even if they don't call it that)
- Their LTV/contract value (gives you ROI calculation inputs)
- Their real bottleneck (leads? conversion? retention?)
- Whether they have budget (indirect signals: team size, current spend)

---

## 3. Pain Amplification (5 min)

Reflect back what they said. Make the cost of the problem real.

```
"So if I'm hearing you right — you're getting [X] clients/month 
right now, you want [Y], and the gap is costing you roughly 
[Z * contract value] per month in unrealized revenue.

Is that about right?"
```

Then silence. Let them confirm or correct. Either way, they've now said the number out loud.

---

## 4. ROI Math (5 min)

Show them the math live. Use the [CAC/LTGP calculator](../02-CLIENT-ECONOMICS/cac-ltgp-calculator.md).

```
"Let me show you how we think about this.

If your average client is worth [contract value] 
and stays for [retention months], that's [LTGP] in lifetime value.

If we get you [N] new clients over [timeframe], 
that's [N × LTGP] in new gross profit.

Our fee is [price/month × commitment]. 
That's a [ROI multiple]x return on investment.

The math either works or it doesn't. For you it looks like [assessment]."
```

**Rule:** If the ROI multiple is below 3x, say so. Don't push a deal that doesn't make sense for them — you'll lose them as a client in 30 days anyway.

---

## 5. Offer Presentation (5 min)

Only present the offer after you've shown the math. Structure:

```
"Here's what we'd do for you specifically:

[Deliverable 1] — [what it produces]
[Deliverable 2] — [what it produces]
[Deliverable 3] — [what it produces]

Timeline: [how long to first results]
Investment: $[price]/month
Commitment: [30-day pilot / month-to-month / quarterly]
[Guarantee if applicable]

Any questions about what's included?"
```

## 7. Close (2–3 min)

After handling objections, make a direct ask.

```
"Based on everything we've talked about — does this feel 
like the right move for [company]?"
```

If yes:
```
"Great. Here's how we get started: [next step — invoice, 
onboarding call, contract link]."

"I'll send that over right after this call. Sound good?"
```

If not ready:
```
"Totally fine. What would need to be true for this to make sense?"
```

Their answer tells you exactly what to address, or confirms it's not a fit.

---

## Pricing Principles

- **Never discount** — discounting signals desperation and devalues your service
- **Never reveal the price before the math** — anchor value first, then price
- **Full price or no deal** — a client who haggles before they start will haggle forever
- Price range for AI agency services: $1,500–$5,000/month retainer
