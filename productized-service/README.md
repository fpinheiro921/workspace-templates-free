# Productized Service

> Build a solo subscription service with fixed scope, flat monthly pricing, and recurring revenue — based on Brett Williams' DesignJoy model.

---

## The Core Insight

**A productized service is a fixed scope of work sold for a fixed price, over and over again — like a physical product.**

Most freelancers and agency owners trade time for money: more clients means more hours, more proposals, more chaos. A productized service breaks that equation. You define what you do once, set a price once, and deliver the same process to every client. Revenue compounds. Complexity doesn't.

**The model in one line:** Clients pay a flat monthly subscription to access a defined scope of your work, delivered on your process, on your tools, on your timeline.

---

## Why It Beats Custom Client Work

| Dimension | Custom / Hourly | Productized Service |
|-----------|----------------|---------------------|
| Revenue predictability | Lumpy, project-dependent | Recurring, monthly |
| Proposals | Every client, hours of work | Never — scope is pre-defined |
| Contracts | Required | Not needed — payments are upfront |
| Scope creep | Constant negotiation | Structurally impossible |
| Onboarding | Weeks of calls | Client self-onboards in minutes |
| Client filtering | Happens after problems arise | Happens at the website |
| Earnings ceiling | Hours × rate | Decoupled from hours |

The website does the selling, filtering, and onboarding. You do the work.

---

## The Model: How It Works

```
CLIENT LANDS ON YOUR SITE
        ↓
Clear scope + pricing (take it or leave it)
        ↓
Self-checkout → payment processed upfront
        ↓
Client added to Trello/Notion → submits first request
        ↓
You deliver → client submits next request
        ↓
Subscription renews monthly (no action needed)
```

**Key structural rules:**
- Payment is always upfront — never invoice, never chase
- Your tools, your process, your timeline — no exceptions for any client
- No meetings, no calls, no Slack (unless it's part of your defined model)
- One active request at a time is standard; queue-based delivery is the norm
- Turnaround defined in the plan (e.g., 2 business days per request)

## Defining Your Packages

### How Many Plans

- **1 plan** — ideal. Easiest to run, easiest to sell. No decision paralysis.
- **2 plans** — acceptable. Must have clear, obvious differentiation.
- **3 plans** — maximum. If you can't explain why someone would choose each one, collapse them.

### What to Differentiate On

Common differentiators between tiers:
- Number of active requests at a time (e.g., 1 vs 2)
- Number of brands covered
- Turnaround speed
- Scope of work types included
- Billing cadence (monthly vs quarterly vs annual)

### What to Include in Each Plan (5–8 items)

Be specific. Vague features destroy trust and create difficult clients.

| Good (Specific) | Bad (Vague) |
|----------------|-------------|
| Unlimited landing page design requests | Unlimited design requests |
| 2 active requests at a time | Fast turnaround |
| 48-hour delivery per request | Quick delivery |
| Webflow development included | Dev support available |
| Pause or cancel anytime | Flexible terms |

**Template feature list:**
- [ ] Unlimited [your service type] requests
- [ ] [X] active requests at a time
- [ ] [X]-day average turnaround
- [ ] [Specific deliverable types included]
- [ ] [Specific deliverable types excluded — or "not included"]
- [ ] [X] brands / seats
- [ ] Pause or cancel anytime
- [ ] Async communication only (no calls)

## Pricing Principles

### The $25K/Month Formula

```
5 clients × $5,000/month = $25,000/month
```

This is the baseline target. Five clients is achievable. $5,000/month is justifiable for any senior-level professional skill.

**Starting out:** Begin at $2,000–$3,000/month if you need to build confidence and a portfolio. Raise prices as demand grows. The goal is $5,000/month per client.

### Price Signals Client Quality

- Low pricing (~$500/month) attracts high-volume, high-maintenance clients with small budgets
- Premium pricing (~$5,000/month) attracts established businesses for whom your fee is a small line item
- Price is positioning — it determines who shows up at your door

### Never Discount

Discounting to win a client signals desperation and sets a precedent. The model works because it's non-negotiable. A client who haggles on price will haggle on scope, process, and deadlines too.

### Refund Policy

- Acceptable: Refund unused time remaining in a subscription if a client cancels early
- Never acceptable: Refund for work already completed
- No free trials — your work has value, put the risk on the client

## Client Acquisition

### You Don't Need a Social Media Audience

A large following accelerates growth but is not required. DesignJoy operated successfully for 5 years before Brett had any meaningful social presence.

**What works without an audience:**

**1. Free communities**
Join Facebook groups, Discord servers, and Slack communities in your target niche. These are chronological, not algorithmic — your posts actually get seen. Show up, provide value, mention your service when relevant.

**2. Your website as the funnel**
A single, clear landing page that answers:
- What do you do?
- Who is it for?
- What does it cost?
- How does it work?
- How do I start?

That page is your sales team, filter, and onboarding system in one. Clients who sign up have already agreed to your terms.

**3. Short qualification calls**
15-minute calls with prospects who are almost ready. Not discovery calls — just confirmation of fit. Most clients won't even need this if your site is clear enough.

### No Paid Advertising Needed

Brett has never spent money on ads. Free platforms, communities, and organic reach built DesignJoy to $25K/month and beyond.

**Launch budget benchmark:**
- Under $1,000 total (website + tools)
- Under $100 if you can build your own site

## Operations: Running It Solo

### The Minimal Stack

| Function | Tool | Cost |
|----------|------|------|
| Request management | Trello (free tier) | $0 |
| Website | Webflow / Framer / single-page builder | ~$29/month |
| Payment / subscriptions | Stripe or equivalent | % of revenue |
| Design / delivery | Your existing tools | Variable |

No custom platform. No proprietary client portal. No Slack workspace per client. Use tools that already exist.

### The Request Workflow

```
Client submits request (Trello card)
        ↓
You pick up the next card in queue
        ↓
Complete and deliver (async — no calls)
        ↓
Client reviews → approves or requests revision
        ↓
Card marked done → you pick up the next
```

One request at a time per client (standard model). Queue-based. Predictable.

### What You Eliminate

- Proposals (scope is pre-defined)
- Contracts (payment is upfront)
- Onboarding calls (clients self-onboard)
- Meetings (async only)
- Custom tool setups per client (your tools, not theirs)
- Scope negotiations (take it or leave it)

Every eliminated step is time you spend on actual deliverables instead.

### Time to Value as Competitive Advantage

Traditional agency: 2–3 months before a client sees any work.
Productized service: 30-second signup → first deliverable in 48 hours.

This alone wins clients. Speed and low friction are features.

## Key Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| **MRR** (Monthly Recurring Revenue) | Total subscription revenue | $25,000/month |
| **Active clients** | Paying subscribers | 5 at $5K/month |
| **Queue length** | Pending requests across all clients | Manageable — never overloaded |
| **Avg turnaround time** | Hours from request pickup to delivery | Per your defined SLA |
| **Churn rate** | % of clients canceling per month | Track and minimize |
| **Capacity utilization** | Are you overloaded or underloaded? | Adjust pricing or capacity |

### The Capacity Warning Sign

If queue length is consistently growing and turnaround is slipping, you have two options:
1. Raise prices to reduce demand
2. Build a team (with all the tradeoffs that entails)

Do not overpromise and underdeliver. Protect the process.

## Launch Checklist

### Before You Launch
- [ ] Define your service scope — exactly what's in, exactly what's out
- [ ] Define your process — tools, turnaround time, communication method
- [ ] Build your pricing (1–3 plans, 5–8 features each, specific not vague)
- [ ] Build a single-page website: scope, pricing, how it works, CTA
- [ ] Set up payment processing (Stripe or equivalent)
- [ ] Set up Trello board for request management
- [ ] Write your "how it works" onboarding doc (1 page max)

### Getting First Clients
- [ ] Join 3–5 free communities in your target niche
- [ ] Post consistently and be genuinely helpful (not just promotional)
- [ ] DM warm leads who ask relevant questions in those communities
- [ ] Offer a brief 15-min call to answer questions for interested prospects
- [ ] Get first client, deliver excellently, ask for a referral

### Never Do
- [ ] Work for free (no free samples, no free trials)
- [ ] Refund completed work
- [ ] Make exceptions to your process for any client
- [ ] Write proposals
- [ ] Sign up for calls or meetings outside your defined model
- [ ] Discount to close a deal
