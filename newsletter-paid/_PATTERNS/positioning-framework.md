# Positioning Framework

## What it is

A concrete process for turning a broad "industry" into a specific, defensible niche — plus the bio language to claim it in 160 characters.

## The pattern

### Step 1 — The 6 ways to niche down

Pick one or combine two. Never start with "I write for everyone."

1. **Sub-industry** — health & fitness → physical therapy
2. **Demographic** — healthcare → healthcare for veterans; healthcare for moms on disability
3. **Experience level** — watch collecting → for beginners; for high-net-worth individuals
4. **Price point** — business consulting → for Series A startups; for $100M companies going public
5. **Problem** — Airbnb rentals → luxury Airbnbs not booking; Airbnbs losing money to maintenance
6. **Location** — therapy → Chicago therapists; Denver therapists

Good positioning usually stacks 2-3 of these: e.g. "copywriting (sub-industry) for Series A SaaS founders (price point + sub-industry) who hate writing (problem)."

### Step 2 — Build the Client Constellation

Niching tighter doesn't shrink your market — it reveals tangentially related niches that share your skills. Map **20 adjacent industries** you could also serve.

Example — "ghostwriter for residential real estate entrepreneurs":
- Mortgage lenders
- Airbnb operators
- Commercial real estate
- Home remodeling services
- Plumbers
- Carpenters
- Interior designers
- Title companies
- Real estate attorneys
- Real estate SaaS founders
- Property managers
- Home inspectors
- Appraisers
- Stagers
- Moving companies
- HOA management
- Real estate podcast hosts
- Flip investors
- REIT operators
- Real estate course creators

Same skills, adjacent buyers. See `client-constellation.md` for the mental model.

### Step 3 — Three bio variants (160 chars max)

Pick the niche + service, then write 3 versions:

- **V1 — plain:** `I {verb} {service} for {industry}.`
- **V2 — tighter:** `I {verb} {specific service} for {1 of the 6 ways to niche}.`
- **V3 — outcome-attached:** `I {verb} {specific service} for {specific niche}, helping {solve specific problem / achieve specific outcome}.`

**Examples (ghostwriter):**
- V1: "I ghostwrite LinkedIn content for SaaS founders."
- V2: "I ghostwrite LinkedIn content for Series A SaaS founders."
- V3: "I ghostwrite LinkedIn content for Series A SaaS founders, helping them attract warm inbound leads without burning 10 hours a week."

V3 is almost always the winner.

## How to apply

### In a positioning-gate `.windsurfrules`

```
Before drafting any content, verify CONTEXT/positioning.md has all 3 parts filled:
1. Primary niche chosen (with which of the 6 ways applied)
2. Client Constellation: ≥20 adjacent niches listed
3. Bio V3 written (industry + specific service + outcome)

If any part is "TBD" or empty, refuse to draft and surface the gap to the user.
```

### In a positioning-generator prompt

Input: broad industry + service you want to specialize in.

Output structure:
1. **30 more specific niches** in that industry, organized into the 6 ways (5 each)
2. **20 tangentially related niches** (Client Constellation)
3. **3 bio variants** (V1/V2/V3)
