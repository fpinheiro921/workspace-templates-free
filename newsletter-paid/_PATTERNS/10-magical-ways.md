# The 10 Magical Ways — Universal Content Expander

## What it is

A short list of 10 content "angles" that work in ANY niche. Use when you have a topic but don't know how to turn it into a post / newsletter / video / landing page section.

## The 10 angles

Any topic + any one of these = a piece of content.

1. **Tips** — `7 tips for {outcome}` — actionable, tactical
2. **Stats** — `3 stats about {topic} that will change how you think about it` — data-driven
3. **Steps** — `The 5-step process to {outcome}` — sequential, ordered
4. **Lessons** — `8 lessons from {experience or case study}` — reflective, narrative
5. **Benefits** — `10 unexpected benefits of {behavior or practice}` — motivational
6. **Reasons** — `5 reasons why {common belief} is wrong` — contrarian, frame-shifting
7. **Mistakes** — `6 mistakes beginners make with {topic}` — avoidance-framed
8. **Examples** — `12 examples of {principle} in action` — illustrative
9. **Questions** — `7 questions to ask before {decision}` — reflective, coaching
10. **Personal Stories** — `What I learned from {specific experience}` — vulnerable, humanizing

## How to combine

### Variant 1 — Apply to one topic

Topic = "niching down for freelance writers"

| Angle | Hook |
|---|---|
| Tips | 7 tips for picking your first profitable niche |
| Stats | 3 stats that prove niching down makes you more money (not less) |
| Steps | The 5-step process to finding your first profitable niche |
| Lessons | 8 lessons from my first 12 months trying to niche down |
| Benefits | 10 unexpected benefits of niching into a "boring" industry |
| Reasons | 5 reasons you're afraid to niche down (and why you shouldn't be) |
| Mistakes | 6 mistakes new freelance writers make when niching down |
| Examples | 12 examples of niche writers earning $5k+/mo |
| Questions | 7 questions to ask before committing to a niche |
| Personal Stories | What I learned from picking the wrong niche for 2 years |

One topic → 10 content pieces.

### Variant 2 — Apply to a 90-day calendar

12 weeks × 1 angle per week = rotating 10 angles + 2 "wildcards" (interviews, polls, tier lists).

### Variant 3 — Mix for longer pieces

A newsletter that combines 3 angles is often the highest-performing format:
> `3 mistakes + 5 steps + 1 story = "The 5-step process I wish I'd known before making these 3 mistakes"`

### Variant 4 — As section headers in long-form

A 1,500-word newsletter can structure its 3-10 section headers around different angles:
- Section 1: a **Story** opener
- Sections 2-4: **Steps**
- Section 5: common **Mistakes**
- Section 6: **Questions** to ask yourself

## Rule for picking an angle

If you're stuck, default to **Mistakes** (highest engagement) or **Steps** (highest actionability). Avoid starting a new writer on **Personal Stories** — hardest to write well, takes practice.

## Alternate Taxonomy — Tim Denning's Personality Angles

The 10 Magical Ways are topic-first (start with a subject, pick an angle). Tim Denning's system is **personality-first** (start with an emotion or stance, find the topic). Use when you want content that feels bold and human rather than informational.

| Angle | Tweet pattern | Thread pattern |
|---|---|---|
| **Bold statement** | `{Topic} can {bold claim}` | `{N} reasons I started {x} that'll inspire you to do the same` |
| **Contrarian** | `Top 1% in {field} hate {thing} and love {contrarian thing}` | `{N} contrarian takes on {topic} that'll leave you speechless` |
| **Mindset shift** | `I used to think {x} was smart. Now I think {y} is genius instead` | `Average people do {x}. Smart people do {y}. Here's how to become one of the smart ones.` |
| **Habits** | `Successful {type} all do {action}` | `{N} habits of successful {type} who all {have this in common}` |
| **Mistakes** | `Biggest mistake I made starting {thing} is I did {action}` | `Biggest mistakes beginner {type} make — avoid them to become {benefit}` |
| **Hard lesson** | `Hardest life lesson I ever learned is {lesson}` | `{N} hardest life lessons I've ever learned` |
| **Brutal truth** | `Most brutal lesson I ever learned is {lesson}` | `{N} brutal truths about {topic} that will help you {benefit}` |
| **Before/after** | `10 years ago I was… 5 years ago I was… Now I'm… {lesson}` | `Why {this matters} and how it's been destroyed by {thing}` |
| **Income story** | `I made {$x} in {timeframe} by {thing}` | `How I made {$x} by {thing} in {timeframe} — each tweet is one step` |
| **Framework** | `Framework turning average → {desirable type} is {name} because {reason}` | `{N} simple frameworks to make you a badass {type}` |
| **Personal rule** | `I have a rule: I will not {x} unless {condition}` | `You've been told {common thing}. This is a lie. The {N} types you MUST understand.` |
| **Harsh truth** | `I don't know who needs to hear this but {harsh truth}` | `Me 5 years ago: [list]. Me today: [list]. Here's the story.` |
| **Anti-hack** | `Fastest way to {goal}: [bad advice list]. This is dumb. Real answer: {advice}.` | `Every {event} has {feature}. But 99% skip it. {N} unique {things} to get {benefit}.` |
| **Old fashioned** | `Call me old fashioned, but I still think {unusual opinion}` | `{N} old fashioned opinions about {topic} truer today than ever` |
| **Audience question** | `In ONE sentence what's one thing about {topic} everyone should know? I'll go first:` | `Story of how I almost {dramatic thing}. Lessons on {topic}.` |

**When to use this taxonomy instead of the 10 Magical Ways:**
- When the post needs a strong point-of-view or personality, not just information
- When you're writing for a personal brand (vs a brand account)
- When recent content has been too tactical — inject one personality-angle post per week to balance
- Contrarian and Mindset Shift angles are highest engagement openers for new accounts

**Tim Denning meta-rules (apply to all angles):**
- Contrarian takes are cool — don't avoid an opinion because it's uncomfortable
- White space between sentences matters — keep it
- Fewer words is better — Twitter is about conciseness
- Single tweets and thread headlines must pack a punch
- These are prompts, not scripts — add your signature style, use a thesaurus, riff on them

## How to apply

### In a prompt

Use as structured scaffolding:
> "Generate 10 content ideas about {topic}, one per angle:
> 1. Tips: {idea}
> 2. Stats: {idea}
> 3. Steps: {idea}
> ...etc."

LLMs love this — it forces diversity across the 10 ideas instead of returning 10 variations of the same hook.

### In a `90-day-calendar.md` file

Structure the calendar as rows for weeks × columns for angles. Each cell = one piece of content.

### In `swipe-file.md` monthly "mirror" ritual

When deconstructing a viral post, identify which of the 10 angles it used. Over time, you'll learn which angles work best for your niche.
