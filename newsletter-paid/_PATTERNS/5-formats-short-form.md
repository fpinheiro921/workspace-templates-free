# 5 Formats for Short-Form Content

Proven structures for high-performing short-form social posts.

**Source:** Adapted from Ship 30 for 30 (Dickie Bush/Nicolas Cole)  
**Applies to:** X/Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram, Threads

## Overview

These five formats consistently outperform generic posting. They work because they:
- Respect platform constraints (280 chars on X, 3,000 on LinkedIn)
- Deliver value quickly
- Create pattern recognition for readers
- Compound across platforms (repurpose easily)

## The 5 Formats

### Format 1: Paragraph Style

A single, tight paragraph presenting a thought-provoking perspective.

**Why it works:** The paragraph format signals "this is thoughtful" simply by looking different from typical fragmented posts.

**Attributes:**
- Concise, economical language
- Alternating sentence lengths for rhythm
- Strong opinion or stance (no hedging)
- Subtle literary devices (alliteration, antithesis)

**Example structure:**
> [Declarative opening]. [Supporting contrast]. [Concluding insight].

---

### Format 2: What / How / Why

A three-part structure that teaches a complete concept.

**Structure:**
1. **What** — Opening declarative line (the hook)
2. **How** — 3–5 bulleted examples, steps, or actions
3. **Why** — One-sentence insight or benefit

**Important:** These labels don't appear in the post. They guide creation, not presentation.

**Example:**
> 4 steps to [outcome]:
> 1. [Action 1]
> 2. [Action 2]
> 3. [Action 3]
> 4. [Action 4]
> *Then, [concluding insight].*

---

### Format 3: Listicle

A compressed list delivering multiple answers quickly.

**Model:** Single sentence promising value → List delivering that value

**30 Proven Listicle Approaches:**
- Tips, Skills, Tools, Traits, Steps
- Goals, Books, Habits, Stories, Quotes
- Secrets, Insights, Benefits, Lessons, Reasons
- Creators, Routines, Mistakes, Podcasts
- Examples, Questions, Challenges, Frameworks

**Example:**
> 7 [category] for [audience]:
> 1. [Item]
> 2. [Item]
> ...

---

### Format 4: Old vs New

Compare two approaches, states, or archetypes.

**Variations:**
- Old way vs New way
- Bad vs Good
- Common vs Uncommon
- Then vs Now
- Reactive vs Proactive

**Example:**
> Most people [old approach].
>
> But the [new approach] is [different action].
>
> [Result/outcome].

---

### Format 5: 10 Magical Ways (Expansion Layer)

Not a standalone format — a set of 10 content angles (Tips / Stats / Steps / Lessons / Benefits / Reasons / Mistakes / Examples / Questions / Stories) that can be layered onto Formats 1–4 to generate variations.

**Canonical reference:** `@h:/WORKSPACE TEMPLATES/_PATTERNS/10-magical-ways.md` (full list, usage patterns, calendar integration).

**Quick rule:** pick any topic → choose 1–3 angles → plug into Format 1–4.

---

### Format 6: Thread Body Structure (Erica Schneider)

For multi-tweet threads and long LinkedIn posts. Each node in the thread follows this 4-part internal structure:

**Structure per tweet/node:**
1. **Header** — Set the stage. Name the concept or point directly.
2. **Subheader** — Answer "the why." Why does this matter right now?
3. **Body** — Supplement the argument. Answer "the how." Data points, personal anecdotes, examples — something the reader can picture themselves doing.
4. **Takeaway** — Empower the reader to act. Give them one reason why following this advice helps them.

**Example (meta — explaining white space):**
> Header: "Answer intent right away"
> Subheader: "This way, the reader understands why what they're about to read matters."
> Body: "Include data points or personal anecdotes so they can picture themselves doing what you're describing."
> Takeaway: "Small tweaks like this make a big impact on flow and drive engagement."

**Thread-specific writing rules:**
- White space is not optional — limit paragraphs to 2–3 sentences max; readers skim heavily
- The hook tweet promises the entire thread's value — don't bury the lead
- Every tweet must stand alone: a reader who sees only tweet #4 should still get value
- Don't include the listicle answers in the hook — make the reader scroll to see more
- The topic and content drive format; not every tweet will have all 4 parts (a transition may replace a takeaway)
- Use your best judgment when ideas overflow between tweets

**Thread types (for brainstorming which angle to use):**
1. Solution to a tough problem
2. Prove the status quo wrong
3. Turn an overwhelming topic into something approachable
4. Tell an emotional story that imparts a lesson
5. Identify key trends on a topic
6. Articulate what everyone is thinking but nobody is saying

## Cross-Platform Application

These formats work across platforms with minor adaptations:

| Platform | Adaptation |
|----------|------------|
| X/Twitter | 280 chars, punchier hooks |
| LinkedIn | 3,000 chars, headers, longer intros |
| Instagram | Carousel format, visual breaks |
| Threads | Thread format for longer pieces |

## Cascade Usage

When creating short-form content:
1. Identify which format fits the idea
2. Follow format rules strictly
3. Compress to platform limits
4. Maintain value density
