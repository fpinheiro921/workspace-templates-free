# Niching Down Framework

A 3-phase approach to finding and owning your niche.

**Source:** Adapted from Ship 30 for 30 (Dickie Bush/Nicolas Cole, 2025)  
**Applies to:** Ghostwriters, creators, service providers  
**Goal:** Move from generalist to high-value specialist

## The 3 Phases

### Phase 1: Your Niche Is Your Network

**Who this is for:** Beginners, those starting out

**The approach:**
- Don't overthink niche selection
- The first person who says "Yes" — that's your niche (for now)
- You don't need:
  - A master niche plan before beginning
  - Perfect online presence
  - Expert status in an industry

**Real example:**  
First client was a 70-year-old gynecologist and menopause expert. Unlikely? Yes. But it was the first Yes. That became the initial niche.

**Mindset:**  
"Whoever says Yes first, that's your niche (for now)."

---

### Phase 2: Pick Something — And Iterate

**Who this is for:** Those with some client experience

**The approach:**
1. Try different client types
2. Notice what you enjoy
3. Notice what makes money
4. Double down on the overlap

**The iteration loop:**
- Enjoy working with a certain client type? → Find more like them
- Enjoy writing in a specific industry? → Write there more
- Enjoy providing a particular service? → Specialize in that service

**For overthinkers:**  
This is the antidote to "I can't because..." narratives. Action beats analysis.

**The rule:**  
Do more of what makes you happy AND money.  
Do less of what makes you unhappy AND doesn't pay.

**It's okay to change:**  
What you like and what pays will evolve. Change with them.

---

### Phase 3: Niche Down (The Goal)

**Who this is for:** Those ready for premium positioning

**Why specialize:**
- Specialists earn more than generalists
- Specialization creates positioning advantage
- Focus gives pricing power
- Expertise builds operational efficiencies

**The specialist advantage:**
| Generalist | Specialist |
|------------|------------|
| Competes on price | Commands premium rates |
| "I can write anything" | "I'm the go-to for X" |
| Constantly proving value | Value assumed |
| Broad marketing | Targeted outreach |

**Overcoming the fear:**  
"Niching down" creates anxiety. Common faulty beliefs:
- "I'll miss opportunities"
- "What if I pick wrong?"
- "I'm not expert enough"

**The truth:**
- Specialists have MORE opportunities (qualified ones)
- You can always pivot
- Expertise comes FROM specializing, not before

---

## The Complete Journey

| Phase | Mindset | Action | Timeline |
|-------|---------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Say yes to first client | Take any aligned work | 0–6 months |
| 2 | Test and iterate | Try variations, track enjoyment/profit | 6–18 months |
| 3 | Commit and own | Become THE specialist in your niche | 18+ months |

## Niching Checklist

**Before committing to a niche:**

- [ ] Worked with 3+ clients in this area?
- [ ] Enjoy the work consistently?
- [ ] Can charge premium rates ($3K+/month)?
- [ ] See clear demand/suffering in the market?
- [ ] Have some proof of results?
- [ ] Can articulate the specific problem you solve?

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | The Fix |
|---------|---------|
| Analysis paralysis | Take the next Yes. Iterate later. |
| Fear of commitment | Commit for 6 months. Reassess. |
| Waiting to be an expert | Expertise comes from doing. Start now. |
| Spreading too thin | Say no to off-niche work. |
| Changing too often | Give each niche 6–12 months. |
