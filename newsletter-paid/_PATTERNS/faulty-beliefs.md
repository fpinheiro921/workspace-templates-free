# Faulty Beliefs — The Positioning Gate

## What it is

Three common fears that stop people from niching down, plus the rebuttals. Used as a gate check before Cascade allows downstream work in a writer template.

## The pattern

### The core insight: "2 clients, not 2,000"

Most writers overestimate how much audience they need. The math:

- Target monthly income: $7,000
- Per-client retainer: $3,500/mo
- Clients needed: **2**

That's it. Not 80. Not 800. Two people willing to pay $3.5k/mo for your service. This radically changes how you think about niche size.

### Faulty Belief #1 — "If I niche down, I can only work with people in that niche"

**Rebuttal:** False. Niching creates a **Client Constellation** (see `client-constellation.md`). "FinTech ghostwriter" attracts crypto companies, financial advisors, mortgage lenders, investment SaaS — all adjacent, all paying. Tight niche = wider surface area of tangential opportunity.

### Faulty Belief #2 — "If I niche down, I limit my opportunities and earn less"

**Rebuttal:** You need 2 clients, not 8,000. Ask yourself: "How many clients do I actually need to hit my monthly revenue target?" Usually 2-5. The constraint isn't niche size — it's your ability to deliver specialized work.

### Faulty Belief #3 — "If I niche down, I'm stuck with that niche forever"

**Rebuttal:** Niching isn't marriage. It's a decision you revisit quarterly. The faster you niche, the faster you either validate or pivot. Freelancers who "stay general to keep options open" spend 2+ years not moving.

## How to apply

### As a `.windsurfrules` gate

```
When the user hesitates to niche down (common signals: "I don't want to limit myself", 
"I can do lots of things", "I'm still figuring out my niche"), surface these 3 faulty 
beliefs + rebuttals. Do not proceed with drafting bio / landing copy / outreach until 
the user picks at least one position.
```

### As a CONTEXT file

`ghostwriter-practice/CONTEXT/faulty-beliefs.md` should have:
1. The 3 beliefs (paraphrased from this pattern)
2. The user's own rebuttals (written when they sit down to do positioning)
3. A "check" question: "Which of these am I secretly still believing? Which one is stopping me today?"

The user fills in their answers. Cascade references these answers when routing forward.
