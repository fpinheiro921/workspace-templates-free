# Evergreen Automation

## What it is

Architecture for building automated content loops that post continuously without manual input. Covers scheduling tool selection, queue sizing, sales post ratios, spam-avoidance rules, and the critical distinction between "set and forget" (stale content) and "evergreen with rotation" (live, varied loop).

---

## The Core Concept

Evergreen automation posts your existing content in a loop — repeatedly, automatically, indefinitely. Once the queue is built, the system runs without you.

**Two approaches — only one works:**

| Approach | What it is | Why it fails or works |
|---|---|---|
| **Set and forget** | Post content once and leave it | Content goes stale. Audience has already seen it. Engagement drops over time. |
| **Evergreen with rotation** | Categorized queue that cycles through variation | Stays fresh because posts rotate. Sales posts don't repeat too quickly. Audience gets consistent value. |

The distinction: set-and-forget is posting a finite list and walking away. Evergreen with rotation is an ongoing loop with category management that keeps the output feeling varied.

## Tool Stack

**SmarterQueue** — primary tool. Supports evergreening (loop posting) and categories. Required features: loop posting + category separation.

**Meet Edgar** — alternative to SmarterQueue. Same core functionality. Use if SmarterQueue is unavailable in your region.

**Any scheduling tool with:**
- Evergreening / loop posting
- Category-based scheduling

## Queue Architecture

### Content queue

Minimum 500 posts per queue before going live. This ensures:
- The loop doesn't repeat content too quickly
- Enough variation to feel like a live account
- Buffer for algorithm-favorable posting frequency (4–6 posts per day)

**Sourcing content for the queue:**
- Quotes from a relevant book, person, or topic your audience follows
- Repurposed evergreen advice posts
- Tips, frameworks, and insights in your niche

**Category structure:**
- Category A: Value/content posts (bulk of queue — 480+ posts)
- Category B: Sales posts (40–50 variants per product)

### Sales post queue

**40–50 different sales post variants per product.** This is not optional — it prevents the platform's spam detection from flagging the account for repetitive promotional content.

Each variant should:
- Promote the same offer
- Use different framing (benefit angle, audience angle, urgency angle, testimonial angle)
- Sound distinctly different even though they point to the same destination

---

## Posting Ratio

**1 sales post per day** when posting 4–6 value posts per day.

This gives approximately a 1:4 to 1:6 sales-to-value ratio. Enough to generate revenue without triggering spam filters or training the audience to skip your posts.

**If posting less frequently:** 1 sales post every 2 days.

## Going Live

1. Build the value queue (500+ posts)
2. Build the sales queue (40–50 variants)
3. Set up SmarterQueue/Meet Edgar categories and posting schedule
4. Go live
5. From a main account with existing followers: repost the new account's posts to give it initial exposure
6. Optional: post on the main account — "Follow [@handle] — it posts [value proposition] daily."

The account grows on its own after reaching ~2,000–3,000 followers. At that point it becomes self-sustaining.

---

## Revenue Model

The loop earns through affiliate commissions or product sales from the daily sales post.

**Prerequisites before launching:**
- Identify a relevant product to sell *before* building the queue
- Confirm the product is highly relevant to the content niche (irrelevant products = near-zero conversion)
- Confirm affiliate program exists or you own the product

Do not build the queue without a clear monetization path.

---

## Maintenance

Once live, minimal ongoing input required:
- Add new value posts occasionally to keep the loop from repeating too fast
- Update sales post variants if the offer changes
- Monitor follower growth — if stuck, cross-promote from main account

Check monthly. The system does not require daily attention.
