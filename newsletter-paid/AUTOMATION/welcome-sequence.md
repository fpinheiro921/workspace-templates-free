# Welcome Sequence — 10-Email Onboarding

Runs automatically when someone subscribes. 10 emails over ~14 days. Sets expectations, delivers early wins, introduces your best content.

## Why 10, why 14 days

- **Too short (3 emails, 3 days):** feels like drinking from a firehose; reader forgets you exist after
- **Too long (20 emails, 30 days):** can't maintain quality; starts feeling automated
- **10 over 14:** every other day cadence → frequent enough to build the habit, spaced enough to feel valuable

Adjust if your niche demands — B2B buyers often want slower (5 emails over 14 days); consumer lifestyle audiences can handle faster.

## The sequence

### Email 1 — Instant (0 min after signup)
**Subject:** `You're in — here's what happens next`

- Thank them
- Set expectations (cadence, format, what they'll get)
- Link to pinned newsletter (see `PUBLISHING/pinned-newsletter.md`)
- Ask ONE reply-worthy question: "What are you hoping to learn from <newsletter name>?"

### Email 2 — +1 day
**Subject:** `<top 3 issues to start with>`

- 3 greatest-hits issues (from your archive)
- 1-line context per issue ("This one is our most-shared — for people who…")

### Email 3 — +3 days
**Subject:** `The one framework everyone asks about`

- Your foundational framework / idea / mental model
- 500-800 words, standalone value
- Not a pitch — pure teaching

### Email 4 — +5 days
**Subject:** `What I wish I'd known <N> years ago`

- Personal story that illustrates your main theme
- 400-600 words
- Vulnerable, tangible, specific

### Email 5 — +7 days
**Subject:** `The tools I actually use`

- 5-10 tools / books / resources you genuinely rely on
- If affiliate, disclose
- Value-first, even the affiliate picks

### Email 6 — +9 days (BRANCHING: offer or story)

**Option A (if paid tier or product ready):**
Introduce the paid offer. Warm pitch, not cold. "You've been getting the weekly free — here's what's behind the paywall, if it's for you."

**Option B (if not yet monetizing):**
Another substantive teaching email. Case study or framework.

### Email 7 — +11 days
**Subject:** `A question I get a lot`

- FAQ-style — answer ONE question your niche audience commonly has
- 400-600 words
- Optional: invite reply for "other questions you're wrestling with"

### Email 8 — +12 days
**Subject:** `One small thing you can try this week`

- Micro-tactical — one specific thing to do this week
- Gives them a quick win
- 200-300 words, shortest email in the sequence

### Email 9 — +13 days
**Subject:** `How I actually work`

- Behind-the-scenes: your process, routine, or workspace
- Builds affinity + humanizes
- 400-600 words

### Email 10 — +14 days
**Subject:** `Welcome to the weekly`

- Transition message: "From here, you'll get <newsletter cadence, e.g. 1 newsletter every Thursday>"
- Reminder of the reply-worthy question from Email 1 — "I still read every reply"
- No pitch, no CTA beyond "talk soon"

## Rules

- **Every email has one clear purpose.** Not 5 calls-to-action in one email.
- **Voice consistency** — all 10 in the same voice (per `CONTEXT/voice-guide.md`)
- **Links tracked** via UTM (which email drives which outcomes)
- **Update quarterly** — greatest-hits list rotates, framework may evolve, the "1 small thing" can be swapped for freshness
- **Never pitch more than once** in the sequence — trust is the goal of email 1-10, not conversion

## Metrics

- Open rate per email (target: 40-60% for first 5, 30-50% thereafter)
- Click-through rate on the pinned newsletter (Email 1): target 20%+
- Reply rate (Email 1 question): target 3-8%
- Unsubscribe rate during sequence: <2% (above = sequence is too long or off-voice)

## Cascade behavior

- When user says "set up welcome sequence", Cascade drafts all 10 emails based on this template + user's specific niche + voice guide
- User copies into platform manually (Substack / Beehiiv / Kit — each has different UX)
- Cascade does NOT configure the actual sending logic
