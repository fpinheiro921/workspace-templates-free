## Pricing logic

Starting rate: $10-30/mo for most niches; $50-99/mo for B2B / professional content.
Annual discount: 2 months free (16-17%).
Founding member: usually 1.5-2x monthly OR locked-in lifetime-lower rate.

## Conversion math

Industry benchmarks:
- Free → paid conversion: 1-3% at steady state
- Paid churn: <5% monthly / <25% annual

At 2% conversion on 1,000 free subs at $10/mo = $200/mo
At 2% on 10,000 = $2,000/mo
At 2% on 100,000 = $20,000/mo

SLOW lane. Don't activate unless committed 12+ months.

## Free ↔ Paid ratio rules

- **Free tier must be genuinely valuable.** Never cripple free to drive upgrades.
- **Paid must be genuinely extra.** Not the same content gated — actually different.
- **Refund policy:** 30 days, no questions, publicly stated.
- **Cancellation:** one-click, same dashboard as subscription.

## Launching the paid tier

1. Soft-launch to existing subs via email (subscriber-list-only for first week)
2. Founding-member pricing capped at 100 (scarcity + early-adopter reward)
3. Announce on social AFTER 100 members (so the marketing has proof)
4. Expect 0.5-2% conversion in launch week; 1-2% steady state after

## Metrics

- Free-to-paid conversion rate (target: 1-3%)
- Monthly paid churn rate (target: <5%)
- MRR growth rate
- Average sub tenure (should rise over time; falling = quality degradation)

## Red flags

- Paid tier engagement < free tier (paid subs not opening → their decision to churn is imminent)
- Conversion rate dropping over time (either free quality went down or paid value went down)
- Churn spike after pricing change (revisit the change within 30 days)

## Cascade behavior

When user proposes a new paid-tier feature, check:
- Does it preserve free-tier quality?
- Is it truly "extra"?
- Does `CONTEXT/monetization-thesis.md` have this planned?

If no to any, flag.
