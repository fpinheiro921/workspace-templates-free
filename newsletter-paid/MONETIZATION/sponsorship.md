# Sponsorship Monetization (Newsletter)

Lane 2. Theory in `_PATTERNS/monetization-lanes.md`. See `creator-studio/MONETIZATION/sponsorship.md` for generic playbook.

## Activate when

- 5,000+ engaged subscribers (open rate ≥35%)
- Or: niche audience with very high willingness-to-pay (B2B tools can sponsor at lower list sizes)
- You can write sponsored copy in your voice without resenting it

## Rate card

| Placement | Pricing | Frequency limit |
|---|---|---|
| Primary sponsor (top of newsletter) | $<X> CPM ($30-50 per 1k subs for typical B2B newsletter) | 1 per issue |
| Mid-newsletter | $<Y> CPM (50-70% of primary) | 1 per issue |
| Deep integration (featured review / case study) | $<Z> flat or 2-3x primary CPM | 1 per month |

**Example at 5k subs, $40 CPM primary:** 5 × $40 = $200 per placement.

## Pricing formula

`Monthly sponsor revenue = subs × CPM_per_k × placements_per_month / 1000`

At 10k subs, $50 CPM, 4 placements/mo: 10 × 50 × 4 = $2,000/mo

## Sponsor-fit checklist

Before saying yes to a sponsor:
- [ ] Audience fit (their customers ARE your readers)
- [ ] Product quality (you'd recommend this to a friend)
- [ ] Budget fit (they can afford to renew, not one-shot discovery)
- [ ] Tone fit (their copy matches your voice potential)
- [ ] No competitor conflicts (check with other recent sponsors)

If ANY fails, don't run the placement. Your list trusts you; a bad sponsor burns that trust for 3-6 months.

## Delivery checklist (per placement)

Pre-send:
- [ ] Ad copy matches my voice (rewrite if given canned)
- [ ] Tracked URL (UTM params)
- [ ] "Sponsored by" disclosure
- [ ] Approved by sponsor

Post-send (within 7 days):
- [ ] Engagement report to sponsor
- [ ] Archive of what was sent + metrics
- [ ] Renewal conversation scheduled

## Metrics

- Fill rate (% of available slots sold): target 60%+
- Average CPM: track by industry
- Renewal rate: benchmark 40%+ for B2B, 25%+ for B2C
- Unsub impact per placement (should be 0; >0.5% = sponsor or copy issue)

## Red flags

- Sponsor asking for copy that doesn't match your voice → walk
- Sponsor asking for non-disclosure → walk (violates trust + often violates law)
- Sponsor asking for exclusivity > 30 days → walk (rarely worth the opportunity cost)
- Subscribers complaining consistently → pause that sponsor, review fit
