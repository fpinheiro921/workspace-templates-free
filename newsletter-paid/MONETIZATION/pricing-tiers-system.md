# Pricing Tiers System + Backend Offer Ladder

> The math behind $100K/year newsletters and how to structure tiers that convert.

**Source:** Ben Adkins — The $100K/Year AI-Powered Newsletter

---

## The $100K/Year Math

Pick a price point. Work backwards to your subscriber target.

| Price Point | Subscribers Needed | Monthly Revenue |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| $10/month | 833 | $8,330 |
| $20/month | 417 | $8,340 |
| $50/month | 167 | $8,350 |
| $100/month | 84 | $8,400 |

**The formula:** Monthly price × Subscribers = Monthly revenue × 12 = Annual revenue

**Key insight:** You don't need thousands of subscribers. You need the right price point for your audience's willingness to pay. A B2B niche at $100/month beats a consumer niche at $10/month on far fewer subscribers.

## The 3-Tier Structure

### Tier 1: Free (Lead Magnet)

**Purpose:** Build trust and grow your list. Not a revenue stream — a pipeline.

- Weekly issue (1x/week)
- Core value, no exclusives
- Lead magnet for paid tier: "Get the full [framework/swipe file/report] by upgrading"
- No paywall friction

**Conversion goal:** 5-15% of free subscribers upgrade to paid within 90 days.

---

### Tier 2: Paid ($10-$50/month)

**Purpose:** Primary recurring revenue. The engine of the business.

- Daily or 3x/week cadence
- Exclusive content not available in free tier
- Community access (Discord, Slack, or comment section)
- Bonus resources (templates, tools, databases)

**Positioning:** "Get the full picture, not just the highlights."

---

### Tier 3: Premium/Annual ($100-$500/year)

**Purpose:** Increase LTV and reduce churn simultaneously.

- Everything in Tier 2
- Annual discount (2 months free — "pay for 10, get 12")
- Bonus content or archive access
- Direct access to creator (monthly Q&A)

**Why annual beats monthly:**
- Annual subscribers churn 60-70% less than monthly
- Cash upfront improves cashflow
- Lower monthly equivalent reduces the decision friction

---

## Pricing Psychology Tactics

**1. Anchor high first** — Show the annual plan prominently. Monthly looks affordable by comparison.

**2. The free trial lever** — 7-14 day free trial removes risk from the first decision. Most trials convert.

**3. Money-back guarantee** — 30-day guarantee stated plainly. Reduces hesitation, rarely gets claimed.

**4. Name your tiers** — "Free" / "Member" / "Founding Member" beats generic tier labels.

**5. The upgrade nudge** — At the end of every free issue: one line pointing to what paid members got that free readers didn't.

## The Backend Offer Ladder

The newsletter is the front door. The real revenue is in what you sell next.

| Offer | Price Range | Purpose |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Newsletter (free)** | Free | Audience building |
| **Newsletter (paid)** | $10-50/mo | Recurring base revenue |
| **Course** | $200-1,000 | Deeper education |
| **Community** | $50-200/mo | Peer connection + retention |
| **Coaching/consulting** | $500-5,000 | Personalized help |
| **Done-for-you** | $2,000-10,000 | Full service |

**Sequencing rule:** Never launch the next rung until the current rung generates consistent revenue.

## The First 1,000 Subscribers Path

**Phase 1 (0-100): Personal Network**
- Email everyone you know personally
- Post on all existing social channels
- Ask 10 people you trust to share it once
- Goal: 100 subscribers in week 1

**Phase 2 (100-500): Content + Social**
- Post niche content on 1-2 platforms with soft CTA to newsletter
- Newsletter swaps with complementary newsletters
- Guest posts in adjacent communities
- Goal: 400 organic subscribers over 60-90 days

**Phase 3 (500-1,000): Paid Acquisition**
- Facebook/Instagram ads at $10-20/day
- Objective: Lead generation (free newsletter signup)
- Retarget free subscribers → push to paid tier
- Target CPL: Under $2 consumer, under $5 B2B
- Goal: 500 paid-acquisition subscribers

## Dickie Bush & Nicolas Cole — Monetization Model Details

*Source: Substack Starter Kit, Module 7*

### The Core Principle: Use All 4 Models

The answer to "which monetization model?" is all of them — sequenced. The paid newsletter is the beginning, not the end. Each model adds a revenue layer without replacing the previous one.

**Sequencing rule:** Validate one model before launching the next. Never launch all four simultaneously.

---

### Model 1: Paid Newsletter — Detailed

**The defensibility thesis:** Subscription revenue recurs monthly without reselling. Each new subscriber is recurring, not a one-time sale.

**Substack fees:** Substack takes 10% + Stripe processing (~2.9% + $0.30/transaction).

| Monthly Price | 100 Paid Subscribers | Net After Fees (~87%) |
|--------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| $5/mo | $500/mo | ~$435/mo |
| $8/mo | $800/mo | ~$696/mo |
| $10/mo | $1,000/mo | ~$870/mo |
| $15/mo | $1,500/mo | ~$1,305/mo |
| $20/mo | $2,000/mo | ~$1,740/mo |

**Pricing rule:** Start at $8-10/month or $80-100/year. Never start below $5 (signals low value). Raise after 100 paid subscribers.

**Annual discount:** Offer annual at 2 months free (e.g., $10/mo = $100/yr). Annual subscribers churn at 1/5th the rate of monthly.

**When to launch paid:** Soft launch (founding-member offer) at 300 free subscribers. Full public launch at 500.

**Founding-member tactic:** First 100 paid subscribers at a discounted rate locked forever. Creates urgency, rewards early adopters, locks in a loyal base.

---

### Model 2: Digital Products — Detailed

**The thesis:** Your newsletter subscribers are customers who haven't bought yet. Every issue builds trust. Digital products convert that trust into a one-time sale with no ongoing commitment.

**3-Tier Digital Product Pricing:**

| Tier | Price Range | Product Examples |
|------|------------|-----------------|
| **Entry** | $7-$27 | Template packs, swipe files, checklists, prompt libraries |
| **Mid** | $47-$197 | Guides, mini-courses, frameworks, toolkits |
| **Premium** | $297-$997+ | Full courses, bootcamps, cohort programs |

**The $167,901 example:** A bootcamp priced at ~$997 sold to 168 buyers = $167,901. The entire buyer list was built via newsletter. Free subscribers are customers — they just need the right offer at the right price point.

**Launch sequence:** Write 3 issues on the topic your product covers. On issue 4, announce the product. The 3 prior issues are the proof of value and warm the audience to buy.

---

### Model 3: Self-Publishing — Detailed

**The thesis:** A newsletter is a book written in real-time. After 50+ consistent issues on one topic, you have manuscript-quality material. Compile, edit, and publish.

**Amazon Quick Reads format:** 10,000-30,000 words. Priced $2.99-$4.99. Positioned as "the fastest way to get the full picture" on your topic.

**Flywheel:** Readers find the book on Amazon → subscribe to the newsletter → some upgrade to paid. The book becomes a permanent subscriber acquisition channel.

**Bundle-in strategy:** Offer the self-published book as a paid subscription benefit. Increases perceived value of the paid tier without raising the price.

---

### Model 4: Services — Detailed

**The thesis:** Services can be offered from day one — no audience size required. The newsletter is the marketing; the service is the revenue.

**"Do it for them" insight:** The most common services that emerge from newsletters — ghostwriting, content strategy, newsletter setup — are cases where readers want the outcome without doing the work. If readers consistently ask "can you do this for me?", that's a service offer.

**Pricing guidance:**

| Service Type | Price Range |
|-------------|-------------|
| Coaching / advising | $200-$500/hour or $2,000-$5,000/month retainer |
| Done-for-you newsletter writing | $1,500-$5,000/month |
| Consulting / strategy | $500-$2,000/hour or project-based |
| Newsletter setup / launch | $1,000-$3,000 one-time |

### 3 Monetization Faulty Beliefs (Dickie Bush)

**Faulty Belief 1: "I need 10,000 subscribers to charge money"**
Reality: 300 engaged free subscribers is enough to validate a paid offer. 100 people paying $10/mo = $1,000/mo. The threshold is engagement quality, not list size.

**Faulty Belief 2: "Charging will make people unsubscribe"**
Reality: Launching a paid tier produces a small wave of unsubscribes — all people who were never going to pay. The subscribers who remain are self-selected as your real audience. Fewer unengaged subscribers improves deliverability and open rates.

**Faulty Belief 3: "My audience won't pay for this"**
Reality: This belief is untested until you make the offer. The cognitive dissonance pattern: "I would pay for a newsletter like mine if someone else wrote it, but I don't believe people will pay for mine." That's imposter syndrome, not market data.

### How to Promote Without Being Promotional

**The "For example..." technique (Nicolas Cole):**

Pattern:
1. Write a standalone valuable Note or newsletter section
2. End with: *"For example, I break this down completely in [Newsletter/Product Name] — [link or 'link in bio']."*

"For example" signals you're giving MORE value, not pivoting to a pitch. Readers feel pointed toward a resource, not sold to.

**3 rules:**
1. The content must be complete and valuable without the CTA
2. Name the thing specifically — not "my newsletter" but "[Newsletter Name]" or "my guide on [specific topic]"
3. One per piece — never stack

### Paid Newsletter Conversion Secrets

**2 Paywall Placement Strategies:**

**Strategy 1: Drop right as "the good stuff" starts**
Write the setup and context freely. Place the paywall exactly when the reader thinks "okay, now tell me the actual thing." The frustration at peak interest is the conversion mechanism.
- Best for: how-to content, case studies, frameworks

**Strategy 2: Announce the asset, then drop the paywall**
Name the specific asset in the free section ("This week's paid section includes the 47-item swipe file + the scoring rubric I use"). Then drop the paywall.
- Best for: templates, swipe files, data-heavy issues

**4-Step Conversion Cheat Sheet:**
1. **Issue quality** — The free section must be your best writing. Weak free content = no upgrades.
2. **Paywall placement** — Use one of the 2 strategies. Too early = readers feel ripped off. Too late = no reason to upgrade.
3. **Asset naming** — "Paid subscribers get the 30-day content calendar template" converts at 10x the rate of "Paid subscribers get more."
4. **Upgrade CTA** — One clear link. Sentence case. No exclamation marks. "Upgrade to access this →" outperforms "CLICK HERE TO SUBSCRIBE!!!"
