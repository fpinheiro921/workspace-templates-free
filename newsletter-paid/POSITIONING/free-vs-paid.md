# Free vs Paid — Revenue Model Decision

The first positioning decision. Everything downstream (cadence, content depth, audience expectations) flows from this.

## The 4 models

### Model A — Free-only, monetize elsewhere

Newsletter is 100% free. Revenue comes from: digital products, services, affiliate, sponsorship.

- **Best for:** most newsletters at 0-1k stage
- **Audience expectation:** reliable weekly value; can unsubscribe anytime no consequence
- **Revenue:** needs 3k+ subs for sponsor viability; services can start Day 1

### Model B — Freemium (free tier + paid tier)

Free newsletter 1x/week + paid tier with more (deeper posts, archive, community, calls).

- **Best for:** 1k+ subs with clear "extra" to gate
- **Audience expectation:** free tier is genuinely valuable (not a teaser); paid tier is worth $10-30/mo
- **Revenue:** needs ~1-2% free→paid conversion; compounds slowly

### Model C — Paid-only (behind-paywall from Day 1)

Everyone pays. No free tier except maybe first 2-3 issues.

- **Best for:** hyper-specific B2B niches with willingness-to-pay; established experts with existing audience elsewhere
- **Audience expectation:** each issue justifies the subscription cost alone
- **Revenue:** highest quality subscribers; limited ceiling without another platform

### Model D — Lead magnet for a bigger offer

Newsletter is free, primarily exists to nurture toward a core product / service / course.

- **Best for:** creators with a flagship offer (course, coaching, SaaS) already running
- **Audience expectation:** reliable value; periodic promotion feels earned
- **Revenue:** attributable to the main offer, not the newsletter directly

## Rule for switching

If you switch models after 6+ months of publishing, expect audience confusion + short-term churn. Switches are possible but costly. Make the original decision carefully.
