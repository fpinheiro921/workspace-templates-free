# Platform Choice — Substack / Beehiiv / Ghost / Kit

The 4 major newsletter platforms. Pick one. Don't run multiple simultaneously as primary.

## Decision matrix

| Factor | Substack | Beehiiv | Ghost | Kit (ConvertKit) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Setup time | 10 min | 20 min | 2+ hours (self-hosted or managed) | 30 min |
| Pricing | Free; 10% of paid subs | Free tier up to 2.5k subs; then $42+/mo | Self-hosted: $0; managed: $9-199/mo | Free up to 1k; $29+/mo |

## Quick decision guide

- **Just starting, 0 subscribers?** → Substack. Built-in Substack Recommendations is the single best thing for a new newsletter.
- **500+ subs already elsewhere (Twitter/LinkedIn audience)?** → Beehiiv or Kit. You'll bring the audience, don't need discovery tools.
- **Already run a product / course?** → Kit. Best automation for nurture → offer flows.
- **Want full control, willing to spend time?** → Ghost. Self-host or managed, no platform lock-in.
- **Want paid subscriptions from Day 1?** → Substack (paid-tier easiest) or Ghost (if you want to own everything).

## Why this is a big decision

- **Audience goes to the platform's list** — migrating means asking subscribers to resubscribe. Churn will be 30-50%.
- **Revenue share compounds** — at 1k paid subs @ $10/mo on Substack = $1,000/mo × 10% = $100/mo to Substack. Over 5 years that's $6,000. On Beehiiv/Ghost/Kit you'd pay ~$50/mo flat = $3,000 over 5 years.
- **Discovery mechanics differ** — Substack Recommendations is real; Beehiiv Boosts require paid spend; Ghost + Kit have near-zero built-in discovery.

## Migration considerations

If later you switch platforms:
- Export subscriber list (always possible — right of portability)
- Re-import to new platform
- Send a "we've moved" email to all subscribers
- Expect 30-50% to not resubscribe (they forget, filter it, change email, etc.)
- Minimum 3 months of growth re-building the list you had

Don't migrate unless you're truly blocked on the current platform. Almost always, discipline > migration.
