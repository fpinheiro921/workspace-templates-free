## The Niche Constellation Theory (Dickie Bush)

**The fear:** "If I niche down, I'll limit my audience."
**The reality:** Specificity creates a nucleus that attracts more people, not fewer.

When you're specific, you become the center of a constellation. Adjacent audiences, industries, and topics orbit your niche and pull readers toward you.

**Example:** A newsletter about "productivity for freelance designers" reaches:
- Freelance designers (primary)
- Productivity enthusiasts who happen to freelance
- Designers at agencies considering going solo
- Tools companies targeting creatives
- Other freelancers curious about design-adjacent workflows

The more specific the nucleus, the stronger the gravitational pull on adjacent orbits. A tighter niche = more adjacent topics that naturally cluster around it.

**Use this reframe when:**
- You're tempted to broaden your niche to "reach more people"
- Your content feels like it's for "everyone"
- You're worried about running out of things to say
