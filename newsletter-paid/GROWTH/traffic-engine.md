# Traffic Engine — Your First Growth Lever

Your existing audience (Twitter / LinkedIn / Instagram / YouTube / podcast) is your first growth engine. Everything else compounds later.

## The baseline flow

```
Social post (Twitter/LinkedIn/etc.)
    ↓ (CTA-able link in bio or pinned tweet)
Landing page (custom or platform-native)
    ↓ (signup form)
Welcome sequence (see AUTOMATION/welcome-sequence.md)
    ↓
Weekly newsletter
```

## If you have an audience elsewhere

### Step 1 — Identify your biggest-audience platform

From `BRAND/platform-choice.md` (if you also run creator-studio) or from raw follower counts.

### Step 2 — Update the CTA

On the biggest platform:
- Bio: link to newsletter landing page (not home page)
- Pinned content: a piece that sells the newsletter
- Every 5-10 posts: one explicit newsletter mention with a good reason to subscribe

### Step 3 — Cross-reference content

Every published newsletter should:
- Be mentioned 2-3 times on social in the following week (via `PUBLISHING/repurposing.md`)
- Include a link WITH value — never "read my newsletter", always "the 5 patterns I found, full writeup here"

## If you don't have an audience elsewhere

### Option A — Start one (slow but durable)

Pick platform per `_PATTERNS/monetization-lanes.md` stage logic. Use `creator-studio/` template if building in parallel. Expect 6-12 months before meaningful newsletter traffic.

### Option B — Direct outreach (fast but low-ceiling)

Hand-write intro to 20-50 people who should subscribe. One-at-a-time, personalized. This gets you to 50-200 initial subs. See `GROWTH/comment-engagement.md` + warm-outreach.

### Option C — Ride someone else's audience (medium)

Guest posts, podcast appearances, Substack Recommendations cross-promotion. Requires some existing credibility or a strong specific hook.

## Metrics to track weekly

- **Subs from social** (if platform tracks): Twitter link clicks → signups, LinkedIn link clicks → signups
- **Subs from landing page direct:** baseline organic signups without attribution
- **Newsletter-to-follower ratio:** <N newsletter subs> / <N social followers>. Target: 5-15%. If <3%, you're not driving to newsletter enough from social. If >20%, your newsletter is very well-positioned — protect that.

## Red flags

- Announcing on social "just published a new newsletter" with no value in the tweet — nobody clicks "just published"
- Driving traffic to newsletter home page, not landing page — new visitors don't know what they're signing up for
- No pinned tweet / featured LinkedIn post with the newsletter offer
