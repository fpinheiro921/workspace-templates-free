# Referral Program

Incentivize your existing subscribers to bring new ones. Best lever at 500-5k subs.

## When to activate

- You have 500+ subs (below that, the referral population is too small)
- Your content is genuinely shareable (you've had manual referrals happen organically)
- You have something to OFFER as reward (not vague "thanks")

## The 3 structures

### Structure 1 — Digital rewards ladder

| Referrals | Reward |
|---|---|
| 3 | Exclusive deep-dive post or e-book chapter |
| 10 | Paid-tier access for 6 months |
| 25 | 1:1 30-min call with you |
| 50 | Custom analysis / audit / intervention |

Use the platform's built-in referral tools if available (Substack doesn't have this — you need SparkLoop or similar; Beehiiv has built-in).

### Structure 2 — Shoutout ladder

Free — no paid product required:
- 3 referrals = your name listed in a "thank you" post
- 10 referrals = full shoutout issue
- 25 referrals = custom paid-tier-style asset (archive access, direct email chain, etc.)

Works if your audience is relationship-driven (early community).

### Structure 3 — Simple "forward this" ask

Every 4th-6th issue, a 1-sentence CTA: "If this was useful, forward to 1 person."

No formal rewards. Just makes the ask. Converts ~2-5% of readers to forwarders.

## Which structure fits

| Your stage | Recommended |
|---|---|
| 500-1k subs | Structure 3 only |
| 1-5k subs | Structure 3 + Structure 1 or 2 |
| 5k+ subs | Structure 1 at scale (with platform tools) |

## Setup (if Structure 1)

Tools (pick one):
- **SparkLoop** — works across platforms, $49/mo+
- **Beehiiv built-in** — included in paid Beehiiv plans
- **Substack** — no native, workaround via SparkLoop
- **Kit (ConvertKit)** — no native; SparkLoop or manual

Implementation:
1. Pick tool
2. Configure rewards tiers
3. Build referral landing page (e.g. yourletter.com/referrals)
4. Add "refer a friend" section to every 4-6th newsletter
5. Email existing top subscribers (the ones who reply / open 20+ issues) and tell them about it personally

## The referral CTA template

Tacked onto bottom of issues:

```
---

One last thing —

If <newsletter name> makes your week, share it with 1 person who'd also get value. Here's your unique link: [yourletter.com/ref/<user>]

Refer 3 people → <reward>
Refer 10 → <reward>
Refer 25 → <reward>
```

## Rules

- Track every referral source
- Honor rewards promptly (within 7 days of earning)
- Don't change rewards structure within 6 months of launch (feels bait-and-switch)
- Announce updates to the program in a dedicated issue, not as an aside

## Metrics

- Subs added via referral / total subs added: target 10-30% at steady state
- Average referrals per referrer: target 2-3 (most referrers refer 1; top 10% refer 5+)
- Referrer retention: are your referrers themselves sticking around? (Bad sign if not.)

## Red flags

- Rewards no one is reaching (lower the tier or improve the reward)
- Everyone gets the first tier but no one the second (too big a jump between tiers)
- Referrals are spammy / low-quality subs (they won't open newsletters)

## Substack Recommendation Engine (Writer-to-Writer)

Distinct from the reader referral program above. This is Substack's built-in feature where writers recommend other writers.

**Stat:** 40%+ of Substack subscriptions come from the Recommendation Engine.

### How It Works
When a reader subscribes to your newsletter, Substack shows them a page of your recommended newsletters. When they subscribe to one, that writer can recommend you back — creating a compounding network.

### 3-Step Setup
1. **Find partners** — Use Substack Settings > Recommendations > Partnership Finder. Filter by niche and size. Target complementary (not competing) newsletters.
2. **Reach out first** — Message the writer before recommending. Introduce your newsletter, express genuine interest, propose mutual recommendation. Most writers say yes.
3. **Add 3-5 and maintain** — Settings > Recommendations > Add. Review quarterly. Replace low-sending partners.

### Partnership Criteria
- Adjacent niche (different enough to not overlap, close enough to share audience)
- Active — publishing at least 2x per month
- Engaged — visible comments, replies, restacks
- Similar audience sophistication

## Promo Post — 5-Section Anatomy (Dickie Bush)

Write this as a standalone newsletter issue when launching or relaunching the referral program:

1. **The Hook** — What you're announcing; frame it as an event, not an admin update
2. **The Why** — Why you're rewarding referrals ("growth lets me invest more in this newsletter")
3. **The How** — Exact steps to refer (the unique link, where it lives)
4. **The Tiers** — Spelled out clearly with rewards at each level
5. **The Leaderboard Tease** — Mention a public leaderboard if using one; name top referrers publicly

## Reward Email — 4-Part Structure (Dickie Bush)

Sent automatically when a subscriber hits a tier:

1. **Celebration** — Acknowledge specifically what they did ("You just sent 3 people to [Newsletter Name]")
2. **Delivery** — The reward, clearly delivered or with one-step claim instructions
3. **Social Proof** — One line about the community they helped build
4. **Next Tier Tease** — "You're [X] referrals away from [Tier 2 reward]"

---

## 3 Referral Mistakes to Avoid (Dickie Bush)

1. **Unclear reward delivery** — If the reader can't figure out how to claim the reward, they give up. Make it automatic or one-step manual.
2. **Rewards with no perceived value** — A free month of a $7 newsletter feels small. Frame the dollar amount AND the exclusive access clearly.
3. **No social proof loop** — The leaderboard and public shout-outs keep top referrers competing. Remove them and the program goes cold in 30 days.
