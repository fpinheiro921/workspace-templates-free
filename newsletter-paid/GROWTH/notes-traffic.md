## The 4-Part Notes System (Dickie Bush & Nicolas Cole)

A systematic framework for Notes as a newsletter growth channel.

### Part 1: Content — 3 Buckets

**Bucket 1: Short-form Original**
Standalone insights, observations, or stories. 1-5 sentences. No link needed.

**Bucket 2: Restacks with Commentary**
Restack another writer's Note with 2-3 sentences of your own take. Notifies the writer, builds relationships, reaches their audience.

**Bucket 3: Newsletter Quotes**
Pull a compelling passage from your latest newsletter. Post it as a Note with a link to the full issue.

### Part 2: Cadence — 2x/Day

**Times:** 10am and 4pm (your audience's timezone)

| Day | 10am | 4pm |
|-----|------|-----|
| **Monday** | Standalone Insight | Restack with Commentary |
| **Tuesday** | Personal Story | Community Invitation |
| **Wednesday** | Standalone Insight | Newsletter Teaser |
| **Thursday** | Restack with Commentary | Newsletter Quote |
| **Friday** | Standalone Insight | Community Invitation |
| **Saturday** | Newsletter Teaser | Restack with Commentary |
| **Sunday** | Personal Story | Standalone Insight |

**Batching:** Sunday night, write 5-7 Notes for the week. Each day, add 1-2 reactive Notes based on what's happening in your niche.

### Part 3: Connection — 3 Strategies

**Value-Add Comment:** When commenting on someone else's Note, add substance — a related insight, contrasting example, or question that deepens the thread. Never "love this!" — it signals you're farming engagement.

**Restack with Commentary:** 2-3 restacks per week minimum. Each notifies the original writer and puts your take in front of their audience.

**Notes 15:** Identify 10-15 writers in your niche. Engage with their Notes daily — genuinely. Over 30 days, you become a recognized name in their audience.

### Part 4: Conversion — CTA Rotation

| CTA Type | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| No CTA (pure value) | 60-70% of Notes |
| Newsletter link ("New issue is live") | 1-2x/week |
| Soft subscribe ("If you're not subscribed...") | 1x/week |
| Breadcrumb ("I broke this down in full...") | 2-3x/week |

**The "For example..." close:** End a Note with *"For example, I break this down completely in [Newsletter Name] — link in bio."* Breadcrumb, not hard sell. Converts better than direct CTAs because it feels like a service.

## Why Substack Notes

### The Opportunity
- Notes is Substack's short-form platform (like Twitter/X)
- Built-in distribution to Substack readers
- Lower competition than Twitter
- Direct path to newsletter subscriptions
- Algorithm favors engagement over followers

### The Numbers
- Top creators get 30-50% of subscribers from Notes
- Single viral Note can add 100-500 subscribers
- Daily Notes activity compounds over time
- No paid ads needed for significant growth

### Key Differences from Twitter/X
- Audience is already newsletter readers (higher intent)
- Less political/controversial content
- Longer character limit (300 vs 280)
- Better threading capabilities
- Direct integration with your publication

## How the Notes Algorithm Works

### Algorithm Factors

**1. Engagement Velocity**
- Likes, replies, restacks in first hour
- Early engagement = wider distribution
- Reply to everyone who engages in first 30 minutes

**2. Follower Reach**
- Your subscribers see your Notes first
- If they engage, it goes to their followers
- Chain reaction potential

**3. Cross-Pollination**
- Engaging with other creators' Notes
- Getting restacked by larger accounts
- Mentioning/tagging strategy

**4. Consistency Signal**
- Daily posters get algorithmic boost
- Quality + quantity both matter
- Missed days reduce reach temporarily

### The Notes Flywheel
```
Post Note → Early Engagement → Algorithm Boost → 
Wider Distribution → New Subscribers → 
More Followers → More Initial Engagement → 
[Repeat]
```

## Notes Content Strategy

### The 70-20-10 Rule

**70% Value Content**
- Insights from your expertise
- Lessons learned
- Industry observations
- Quick tips and frameworks

**20% Engagement Content**
- Questions to audience
- Polls and surveys
- "What do you think?" posts
- Fill-in-the-blank prompts

**10% Promotional Content**
- Link to latest newsletter
- Mention paid tier benefits
- Tease upcoming content
- Share wins and milestones

### 7 High-Performing Note Types

**1. The Insight Bomb**
- One powerful idea
- 1-3 sentences max
- Counterintuitive or surprising
- No links (keep on platform)

Example:
```
The best time to launch a paid newsletter?

When you have 300 free subscribers.

Not 1,000. Not 10,000.

300 engaged readers is enough to validate 
your offer and fund your growth.
```

**2. The Listicle**
- 3-5 numbered items
- Each line is a complete thought
- Save the best for last
- No external links

Example:
```
5 newsletters that made $1M+ in 2023:

1. Lenny's Newsletter (product)
2. Ben Thompson (tech strategy)
3. Heather Cox Richardson (politics)
4. Matt Stoller (antitrust)
5. Doomberg (finance)

The common thread: They all own a category.
```

**3. The Observation**
- Notice something others miss
- Add your take
- Keep it timely
- Reference current events tastefully

**4. The Question**
- Open-ended
- Related to your niche
- Encourages replies
- You reply to build threads

Example:
```
What's the biggest mistake you made
in your first year of [your niche]?

I'll start: [share yours]
```

**5. The Quote/Image**
- Screenshot of interesting content
- Your commentary below
- Visual stops the scroll
- Credit the source

**6. The Story Thread**
- Personal story in 3-5 Notes
- Each Note is one part
- First Note hooks
- Last Note has call to action

**7. The Hot Take**
- Contrarian opinion
- Backed by experience
- Invites discussion
- You engage with respectful dissent

## Common Notes Mistakes

### 1. Too Many Links
- Notes with links get less distribution
- Save links for comments
- Or post without link, add in reply

### 2. Posting and Ghosting
- Reply to every comment in first hour
- Engagement begets engagement
- Algorithm rewards conversation

### 3. Only Promoting Newsletter
- 90% value, 10% promotion
- If every Note is "read my post," you lose followers
- Give freely, gain trust, then convert

### 4. Ignoring Trends
- Timely content gets boosted
- Newsjack relevant industry news
- Add your unique take

### 5. Inconsistent Posting
- Algorithm favors regular posters
- Daily > weekly in reach
- Build the habit before optimizing

### 6. Not Restacking Others
- Restack = Substack retweet
- Generosity is rewarded
- Build relationships with other creators

### 7. Wrong Tone
- Notes are casual, conversational
- Not formal like long newsletter
- Write like you text a friend

## 5 Notes Mistakes to Avoid (Dickie Bush & Nicolas Cole)

**Mistake 1: Treating Notes like Twitter**
Notes are newsletter-native. The audience has opted into long-form reading. They respond to depth and nuance, not hot takes and rage-bait. Shock content performs worse on Notes than on Twitter.

**Mistake 2: Only posting links**
Notes with links receive less algorithmic distribution. Post the Note as pure text. Put the link in the first comment immediately after posting — never in the Note body.

**Mistake 3: Disappearing after posting**
The first 30 minutes after posting determine reach. If you don't return to reply to early comments, engagement velocity stalls and the algorithm caps distribution. Reply to everyone within the first 30 minutes.

**Mistake 4: No engagement with others**
Notes is a conversation platform, not a broadcast platform. Writers who only post and never comment become invisible. Spend at least 15 minutes per day engaging with other writers' Notes before posting your own.

**Mistake 5: No conversion mechanism**
Notes without any subscriber conversion path is pure brand-building with no compounding return. At minimum: your bio links to your newsletter. Optimally: 1-2 Notes per week contain a soft breadcrumb ("I break this down fully in [Newsletter Name] — link in bio").
