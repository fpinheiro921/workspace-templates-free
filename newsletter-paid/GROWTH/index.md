# GROWTH/

Course Module 4. How new subscribers actually find and stay with your newsletter.

## The 5 growth levers

| Lever | Stage fit | Effort | Cost | Speed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Traffic engine (your existing audience / other platforms) | 0-∞ | Low (you already write) | $0 | Slow to fast |
| Cross-promotion | 100+ subs | Medium (relationship work) | $0 | Fast once relationships are real |
| Referral program | 500+ subs | Low setup, medium maintain | $0 or low (rewards) | Medium |
| Paid acquisition | 1k+ subs + monetization | Medium | $100-1000+ per 1k subs | Fastest, highest risk |
| Comment engagement | 0-∞ | Medium (1h/day) | $0 | Slow-medium, compounds |

## Read order (sequential by stage)

- **0-500 subs:** `traffic-engine.md` → `comment-engagement.md`
- **500-5k subs:** add `cross-promotion.md` → `referral-program.md`
- **5k+ subs:** consider `paid-acquisition.md`

Don't skip stages. Paid acquisition without organic foundation = you're renting subscribers, not earning them.

## Rules

- **One lever at a time.** Layer after each is stable.
- **Measure per lever.** Track sub-adds per week attributable to each.
- **Drop losers.** If a lever produces <10 subs/month after 90 days of effort, cut it or reduce to 10% effort.

## Cascade behavior

- When user asks "how do I grow?", surface current stage (from `CONTEXT/newsletter-brief.md`) then recommend next-stage lever
- Refuse to propose paid acquisition if user is <1k subs OR has no monetization lane active (can't afford reader churn)
- Weekly in Monday morning Cascade: "Any growth-lever metrics to update this week?"
