# Comment Engagement — Growth Through Showing Up

Commenting on other people's content is the most under-used growth lever for newsletter creators. This file is the newsletter-specific view.

Full playbook: `creator-studio/OUTREACH/comment-engagement-playbook.md` (cross-template reference). Read that first.

## Why it matters for newsletters specifically

- Your audience is ALREADY reading someone in your niche — be there first
- A single substantive comment on a newsletter with 10k subs can drive 50-200 subs to your own
- Newsletter commenters tend to be higher-signal (they subscribe, they read fully, they engage)
- Builds relationships with publishers in your constellation → cross-promotion opportunities (see `cross-promotion.md`)

## The targets (3 tiers)

### Tier 1 — Publishers you'd love to cross-promote with
3-5 publishers, slightly bigger than you, in adjacent niches. Comment on every issue they publish. Make them know your name.

### Tier 2 — Peers at your stage
10-20 publishers at roughly your sub count. Comment on 50% of issues. Mutual engagement friendly.

### Tier 3 — Giant publications that overlap your audience
5-10 big publications whose readers you'd love. Comment on 1-2 issues/week of the highest-engagement posts.

Maintain list in `POSITIONING/competitive-analysis.md`.

## Where to comment

- **Substack:** Comments section on each post (everyone sees them)
- **Beehiiv:** Comments if enabled (fewer do)
- **LinkedIn newsletter:** the LinkedIn post that announces the newsletter (most engagement here)
- **X/Twitter:** replies to the tweet that announces the newsletter
- **Reddit:** if a newsletter gets posted to a relevant sub

## The 5 formats

From `creator-studio/OUTREACH/comment-engagement-playbook.md`:
1. Counterpoint
2. Listicle Extension
3. Unique Stat
4. Old vs New
5. Mistakes Extension

Rotate. Don't always counterpoint.

## Newsletter-specific tweaks

- **Sign off with your newsletter name + 1-line description.** Once per comment thread, not every comment.
  Example: *"— [your name], writes <newsletter name> for <who>"*
- **Never link-drop in first reply.** Build up value across 3-5 comments first.
- **When the publisher replies back, keep going.** Their engagement is the conversion event.

## Cadence

Daily 30 min minimum:
- 5 comments on Tier 1 (highest priority)
- 3-5 comments on Tier 2
- 1-2 comments on Tier 3 if bandwidth

## Metrics

- Publisher replies to your comments: target 20%+ by month 2
- New subs traceable to comment activity (hardest to measure — use monthly sampling by checking "where did you hear about us?" in welcome survey)
- Cross-promotion conversations started (track in `POSITIONING/competitive-analysis.md`)
