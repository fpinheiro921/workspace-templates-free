# Paid Acquisition

Renting subscribers until you stop paying. Only activate at 1k+ subs WITH active monetization.

## Prerequisites (all must be true)

- [ ] 1,000+ organic subscribers
- [ ] Monetization active (you can quote a subscriber LTV)
- [ ] Open rate ≥40% (subs you added aren't being squandered)
- [ ] Welcome sequence working (from `AUTOMATION/welcome-sequence.md`)
- [ ] You can afford to burn $500-2k on learning which channels work

If ANY is false: not ready. Go back to organic levers.

## The 3 main channels

### Channel 1 — Substack Boosts (Substack-native)

Pay for recommendations from other Substack publications.

- **Cost:** ~$2-8 per subscriber
- **Quality:** high — these subs actively clicked through
- **Scalability:** limited to publications using the Boost system
- **Stage fit:** 500-10k subs

### Channel 2 — Beehiiv Boosts (Beehiiv-native)

Similar to Substack Boosts. Beehiiv's marketplace of boost-providers.

- **Cost:** ~$3-6 per subscriber
- **Quality:** medium-high
- **Scalability:** larger pool than Substack's
- **Stage fit:** 500-20k subs

### Channel 3 — SparkLoop (any platform)

Marketplace where other publishers recommend you in exchange for a fee per sub.

- **Cost:** ~$2-5 per subscriber
- **Quality:** varies — check referral source
- **Scalability:** unlimited
- **Stage fit:** 1k-50k subs
- **Tool cost:** $49/mo+

### Channel 4 — Direct paid ads (Meta / X / LinkedIn)

Run paid ads to a newsletter landing page.

- **Cost:** $5-30 per subscriber (most creators fail here)
- **Quality:** highly variable
- **Skill required:** HIGH — ad copy + audience targeting + landing page optimization
- **Stage fit:** not recommended unless you have paid-ad expertise

## The math

Before spending $1:

- Compute subscriber LTV: average paid-tier conversion × avg subscription length × monthly price × gross margin
- e.g. 2% of subs convert to paid at $10/mo, average 8-month tenure, 90% margin = $1.44 LTV per free sub
- Your CPA (cost per acquisition) must be LOWER than LTV
- At $1.44 LTV, a $5 CPA means you LOSE $3.56 per sub — only works if you also have a backend offer

For most newsletters at 1-5k subs: don't run paid unless your LTV/CPA math works with a 2x margin.

## Testing protocol

1. **Pick ONE channel** (not 3)
2. **Spend $100-300** as a test
3. **Measure after 30 days:**
   - Cost per sub added
   - 30-day open rate of new subs vs organic subs
   - 30-day unsub rate of new subs
4. **Go / no-go:**
   - Go: scale the channel, re-evaluate at $1k spend
   - No-go: try another channel or return to organic

## Rules

- **Budget caps.** Set monthly spend cap before launching; never exceed without decisions-log entry.
- **Never run two channels simultaneously until one is proven.** You won't know which is working.
- **Track UTM on every link.** Without attribution, paid is wasted spend.
- **Reinvest profits only.** Never take from savings to fund newsletter growth — if the org lane can't pay for itself, you're growing the wrong way.

## Red flags

- CPA rising faster than LTV over 3 months
- Paid subs unsubscribing within 30 days at >50% (they weren't really in your niche)
- You're spending more than you're earning and you "believe in the compound" — 6-month rule: if you're not profitable by month 6 of a channel, it's not compounding.
