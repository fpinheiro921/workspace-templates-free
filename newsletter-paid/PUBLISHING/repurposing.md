# Repurposing — 1 Newsletter → N Short-Form Pieces

Every published newsletter should produce derivatives for at least 2 other platforms. Same writing session, 3x the distribution.

## The default matrix

For every published newsletter, produce:

| Platform | Count | Format | Time to create |
|---|---|---|---|
| Twitter / X | 3-5 tweets | 1 per short-form format (Paragraph / What-How-Why / Listicle / Old-vs-New / 10 Magical Ways) | 20 min |
| LinkedIn | 1-2 posts | ≤2,800 chars, picking one sub-idea from the newsletter | 15 min |
| Newsletter pullquote | 1 | 50-word highlight for reuse later | 2 min |

Optional for higher-volume operators:
- Short video script (60-90s): 10 min
- Instagram caption: 10 min

## The workflow

1. Newsletter published Thursday 8am
2. Same Thursday 9am: run `PROMPTS/generators/repurposing-prompt.md` with the full text
3. Review derivatives, edit for voice
4. Schedule derivatives over next 5-7 days (stagger across platforms)

Total: ~45 min per week to 3-5x your distribution.

## Rules

- **Every derivative stands alone** — a reader seeing only the tweet should get value. No "full post in newsletter" CTA (that's audience-beg).
- **Different angle per derivative** — 3 tweets from 1 newsletter = 3 DIFFERENT takes, not 3 paraphrases
- **Preserve tangible examples + numbers** from the source — those are what make it believable
- **Match voice** to the source — if the newsletter is conversational, derivatives are conversational
- **Don't derive from flops** — wait 48h to see engagement; only repurpose winners

## What to repurpose from a single newsletter

Good source newsletter has 3-5 "nuggets" — each worth its own tweet or LinkedIn post. Identify nuggets BEFORE drafting derivatives.

**Nugget types:**
- One-liner that captures the big idea (→ tweet: Paragraph format)
- Specific stat or data point (→ tweet: standalone fact)
- Step-by-step snippet (→ tweet: Listicle)
- Contrast / dichotomy (→ tweet: Old-vs-New)
- Story moment (→ tweet: vulnerable hook)
- Sub-framework (→ LinkedIn post)
