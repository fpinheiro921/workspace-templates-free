# S.L.I.D.E Newsletter / Article Template

A 5-part structure for thought-leadership newsletters that showcase authority, teach something new, and convert readers into paid subscribers or clients.

**Source:** Adapted from Ship 30 for 30 — Society of Storytellers bonus (Parker Worth)  
**Use case:** Authority-building newsletters, long-form articles, sales emails that teach  
**Length target:** 600–1,200 words

## The Acronym

| Letter | Step | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **S** | **S**top the scroll | Attention-grabbing opening |
| **L** | **L**abel the problem | Make the reader aware of a pain |
| **I** | **I**llustrate credibility | Prove you can solve it |
| **D** | **D**emonstrate the solution | Teach — but not fully |
| **E** | **E**nd with a new problem + CTA | Pivot to the bigger problem only you can solve |

## Section Templates

### S — Stop their scroll

Lead with one of:
- A powerful quote
- A specific statistic
- A rhetorical question
- A contrarian declaration

Then add 1–2 sentences of context.

**Example openers:**
> "67% of newsletter writers quit before issue #50."
>
> "What if everything you've heard about email open rates is wrong?"
>
> "Most ghostwriters stay broke forever. Here's why."

---

### L — Label the problem

Make the pain clear. Use one of these frames:

- "But here's the problem..."
- "Most people do X wrong"
- "The hardest part about X is Y"
- "We all know it's not as simple as X"

Be specific. Generic problems create generic reactions.

---

### I — Illustrate your credibility

Establish authority without bragging. Template:

> "Over the past [time period], I've been able to [solve problem / achieve result] by doing [approach]. And I've learned that you need to..."

**Rules:**
- Use real numbers
- Reference real outcomes
- Keep it to 2–3 sentences
- Transition into teaching

---

### D — Demonstrate how they can solve it

This is the teaching section. Structure options:

**Option A: Step-by-step**
1. Step 1 (what + why)
2. Step 2 (what + why)
3. Step 3 (what + why)

**Option B: Framework breakdown**
- Component 1
- Component 2
- Component 3

**Option C: Contrarian reframe**
- Common approach
- Why it fails
- Better approach

**CRITICAL RULE:** Do NOT solve the whole problem. Solve enough that the reader gets value + sees your expertise, but leaves the *bigger* problem for your CTA.

---

### E — End with a new problem + CTA

The reader now has the "small win" from section D. Now pivot to the bigger problem:

> "But here's the thing — [teach X] is only half the battle. The real challenge is [bigger problem]."

Then deliver the CTA. Options:

| CTA Type | Use When |
|---|---|
| Book a call | High-ticket service |
| Click to landing page | Digital product |
| Reply to this email | Community/engagement |
| Share the post | Growth play |
| Comment with your answer | Social proof |

**Rule:** ONE CTA per newsletter. Not two. Not three. One.

## Full Example Skeleton

```markdown
# [Subject line: hook-worthy]

**S:** "Most ghostwriters never break $2K/month."

Here's why — and what the top 1% do differently.

**L:** The problem isn't your writing skill. It's your positioning.

Most writers say "I can write anything for anyone." And the result is no one thinks of them first when they need help.

**I:** Over the past 3 years, I've helped 40+ ghostwriters 10x their rates. The pattern is always the same: they went from generalist to specialist.

**D:** Here's the 3-step specialization process:

1. **Audit your existing clients** — which ones energized you AND paid well?
2. **Pick the overlap** — that's your niche seed
3. **Commit for 90 days** — no off-niche work

**E:** But here's the harder truth: specializing doesn't solve pricing.

You also need an offer stack that positions your service as 10x more valuable than hourly writing.

That's what I cover in my [Premium Ghostwriter Playbook]. Click here to get it →
```

## When to Use SLIDE

| Scenario | Good Fit? |
|---|---|
| Weekly value newsletter | ✅ Yes — add CTA to lead magnet |
| Launch sequence | ✅ Yes — SLIDE × 3 = nurture-to-sale |
| Cold outreach email | ⚠️ Too long unless warm |
| Personal essay | ❌ No — use Personal Essay template instead |
| Tactical how-to | ⚠️ Combine with Tactical How-To template |
