# Writing Templates — 5 Proven Newsletter Structures

Pick one before drafting. Don't improvise.

## Template 1 — Tactical How-To (800-1,200 words)

**When:** teaching a specific skill or process.

Structure:
1. Subject line (from `subject-lines.md`, "steps" or "tips" angle)
2. Greeting ("Hey there!" or your equivalent)
3. Hook — 1-sentence problem statement
4. Promise — 3-5 sentences or bullets on what they'll know by the end
5. Transition — "Let's walk through each one."
6. Sections 1-N — each: header as sentence-style → opener → 3-5 bullets or sentences → one-line closer
7. Closing — 1-2 sentences of synthesis OR a clear CTA

**Rule:** alternate bullet-style and paragraph-style between sections.

## Template 2 — Contrarian Take (1,000-1,500 words)

**When:** strong opinion against a widely-held belief in your niche.

Structure:
1. Subject line — controversial framing ("Stop X. Do Y.")
2. Steelman the common belief (2-3 sentences — state it fairly)
3. The turn — "Here's what's actually true."
4. 3-5 sections — each one piece of evidence for your counter-claim
5. Closing — ONE specific thing the reader should do differently Monday morning

**Rule:** steelman, never strawman.

## Template 3 — Personal Essay (800-1,200 words)

**When:** hard-won lesson from your own experience.

Structure:
1. Subject line — moment-in-time ("Three years ago, I…")
2. Scene — 2-3 sentences putting the reader in the moment (tangible details)
3. The mistake / turning point
4. The aftermath (the cost)
5. The lesson (what you know now)
6. Application — how the reader applies it to themselves

**Rule:** tangible > abstract. "$40k in debt" > "struggling financially."

## Template 4 — Curated Insight (800-1,200 words)

**When:** you've consumed 5+ things on one topic and have a synthesized take.

Structure:
1. Subject line — "What I learned from [source]"
2. Context — what you consumed + why
3. 3-5 sections — each: quoted idea + your interpretation + your application
4. Synthesis — the pattern across all sources

**Rule:** never just quote. Always add interpretation + application.

## Template 5 — Framework Breakdown (1,200-1,500 words)

**When:** you have a model or mental framework to teach.

Structure:
1. Subject line — "The X framework for Y"
2. The problem the framework solves
3. Framework name + 1-sentence definition
4. Each component as its own section (with example)
5. One full worked example using the complete framework
6. Invite the reader to apply it

**Rule:** a framework needs ≥3 components. If it's 2 parts, it's a dichotomy, not a framework.

## Rules across all 5

- Every template uses a hook from `_PATTERNS/hook-library-core.md` (part C — 6 hook types)
- Every piece passes Two-Pass Review before scheduling
- Target word count is a range; going over by 20%+ means cut
- Closing always either: (a) synthesizes, (b) CTAs, or (c) asks a reply-back question. Never two of those in the same closing.

## Template selection by angle

| Angle (from `_PATTERNS/10-magical-ways.md`) | Best-fit template |
|---|---|
| Tips / Steps | Tactical How-To |
| Stats | Contrarian Take or Framework Breakdown |
| Lessons / Personal Stories | Personal Essay |
| Benefits | Tactical How-To or Curated Insight |
| Reasons / Mistakes | Contrarian Take |
| Examples | Curated Insight |
| Questions | Personal Essay or Framework Breakdown |
