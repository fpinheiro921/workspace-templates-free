# Welcome Email

> The automated email sent to every new free subscriber. Triggers on sign-up. Write it once; it runs forever.

**Source:** Dickie Bush & Nicolas Cole — Substack Starter Kit, Module 3

---

## Purpose

The Welcome Email is the first thing a new subscriber reads. It must:
- Confirm they made the right choice
- Re-state the value (in case the landing page wasn't enough)
- Set expectations (cadence, format, what's coming)
- Drive one action (reply, read the archive, forward)

It is NOT a long pitch. It is NOT another chance to sell them on subscribing — they already did. It is a first conversation.

---

## Subject Line Options

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Confirmation + orientation | "You're in — here's what happens next" |
| Welcome + start here | "Welcome to [Newsletter Name] — start here" |
| Simple confirmation | "You're subscribed to [Newsletter Name]" |
| Curiosity + benefit | "One thing most new subscribers miss" |

**Rule:** Sentence case only. No exclamation marks in the subject line.

---

## The 6-Section Template

### Section 1: Confirmation + Warmth (2-3 sentences)

```
Hey [First Name],

You're in. Welcome to [Newsletter Name] — I'm genuinely glad you're here.

Every [day], I send [one-sentence description of the tangible asset].
```

Avoid "Thanks for subscribing!" — it's generic and wastes the first line. Lead with something that signals you're different from the other newsletters in their inbox.

---

### Section 2: Who This Is For (3 bullets)

```
Quick check — this newsletter is for you if:
- [Archetype 1: specific job/role + situation]
- [Archetype 2: specific goal or aspiration]
- [Archetype 3: specific problem they're experiencing]

If that's you, you're in the right place.
```

Readers who recognize themselves in the archetypes become more engaged. It's a micro-commitment — "yes, that's me" — that increases open rates on future issues.

---

### Section 3: What to Expect (the asset, named)

```
Here's exactly what you'll get:
- [Asset 1 — named and specific, not "insights"]
- [Asset 2]
- [Asset 3]

This isn't a newsletter full of opinions. It's a [weekly tool] for [outcome].
```

Name the asset. "The Freelance Rate Card" converts better than "pricing tips." People pay for tools, not essays.

---

### Section 4: Start Here (3 links)

```
If you want to hit the ground running, start with these 3 issues:

→ [Title] — [One-line description of what they'll get]
→ [Title] — [One-line description]
→ [Title] — [One-line description]
```

**If under 10 issues:** Skip this section entirely. Do not link to weak content. Use a placeholder:
```
I'll point you to my best issues once I have a proper archive to recommend.
In the meantime, the next issue lands [day].
```

---

### Section 5: One Ask

```
One thing I'd love you to do right now:

[Single CTA]
```

**CTA options (pick one — never stack):**

| Goal | CTA |
|------|-----|
| Engagement | "Reply and tell me: [specific question]. I read every reply." |
| Growth | "Forward this to one person who deals with [niche problem]. That's the best way to help this newsletter grow." |
| Revenue | "If you want the full [asset] — not just the free version — [upgrade link] gets you there." |

The reply CTA is highest-leverage at launch. Every reply signals to email providers that your emails are valued, improving deliverability across your entire list.

---

### Section 6: Sign-Off + P.S.

```
Talk soon,
[Your name]

P.S. [One-line teaser about the next issue OR a soft paid mention]
```

**P.S. options:**
- **Next issue tease:** "Next [day], I'm breaking down [specific topic]."
- **Paid soft mention:** "If you want [specific paid benefit], [upgrade link] — but no pressure. The free issues already give you [specific value]."
- **Archive orientation:** "The single best issue I've ever written is [Title] — it's in the archive now."

## Full Example (Write With AI — Nicolas Cole)

**Subject:** You're in — here's what happens next

Hey [First Name],

You're in. Welcome to Write With AI — I'm genuinely glad you're here.

Every Thursday, I send one framework for using AI to write faster, better, and more like you — not like a robot.

Quick check — this newsletter is for you if:
- You're a writer who's tried AI and felt like the output sounded nothing like you
- You want to use AI as a creative partner, not a replacement
- You're spending more time editing AI slop than writing your own work

Here's exactly what you'll get:
- One AI writing framework per week (not tips — a complete, named process)
- A prompt you can copy and use immediately
- An example of the framework applied to a real piece

This isn't a newsletter full of "here's what I think about AI." It's a weekly workshop for writers who want to write more without writing worse.

One thing I'd love you to do right now:

Reply and tell me: what's the one thing you most want to learn about writing with AI? I read every reply and it shapes what I cover next.

Talk soon,
Nicolas

P.S. If you want the full prompt library (47 prompts for different writing formats) — that's behind the paid tier. But the free issues will already change how you write.

## Setup Instructions (Platform-Specific)

| Platform | Where to Set It Up |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Substack** | Settings > Email > Welcome Email. Toggle on, write in the editor. Sends automatically on sign-up. |
| **Beehiiv** | Automations > Welcome Email. Triggers immediately on subscription. |
| **Ghost** | Portal > Email customization. |
| **Kit (ConvertKit)** | Automations > Add trigger "Subscribes to form" > Send email. |

**Substack note:** The Welcome Email is separate from the Welcome Pinned Post. Both should exist. The email lands in the inbox; the pinned post lives on the publication homepage.

---

## Optimization Rules

1. **Write it before you launch** — Every subscriber gets it. It is your highest-leverage automated touchpoint.
2. **Read it aloud** — If it sounds like a corporate onboarding document, rewrite it.
3. **No images** — Text-only emails have higher deliverability and feel more personal.
4. **Test the reply CTA** — Even 10% reply rate from your first 100 subscribers gives you 10 conversations about what your audience wants.
5. **Update it at milestones** — Refresh at 500 subscribers (update archive links), at paid launch (update P.S.), when cadence changes.
