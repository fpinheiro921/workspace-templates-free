# Pinned Newsletter — The Cornerstone

The ONE newsletter that sits at the top of your archive forever. First thing a new subscriber sees. Should do the work of 20 lesser issues.

## Purpose

- Explains what the newsletter is, who it's for, what they'll get
- Sells the archive (why past issues are worth reading)
- Sets expectations (cadence, format, tone)
- Showcases your best writing (it IS the sample)
- Ends with a clear next action (paid upgrade / key issue to read / how to reply)

## Structure

### Opening — "Welcome" re-framed (50-100 words)

Don't say "Welcome to my newsletter." That's the platform template.

Say: who YOU are (1 line), who THEY are (1 line), why THIS newsletter (1 line).

### The Why (200-300 words)

Why does this newsletter exist? What hole in the world does it fill? What will they NOT get here (important — sets expectation to avoid wrong subscribers)?

Use your information advantage (from `CONTEXT/newsletter-brief.md`) to justify the right to write this.

### What you'll get (bulleted, 4-7 bullets)

Specific outcomes. Same style as Variant 2 of `POSITIONING/description.md`.

### How it works (100-150 words)

- Cadence ("Every Thursday at 8am EST")
- Format ("1,000 words, 5-minute read, always one tactical takeaway")
- Paid tier (if applicable — what's behind it)
- How to reply / interact with you

### 3-5 greatest-hits issues to start with

Link to your 3-5 best past issues. If you're brand new, skip this section for now — fill it in at 10+ issues.

### CTA

One ask. Options:
- **At launch:** "Forward this to 1 person you think would love it." (Growth play)
- **With paid tier:** "Upgrade here — first 100 subscribers at founding-member pricing." (Revenue play)
- **For engagement:** "Reply with your biggest challenge in [niche]. I read every one." (Connection play)

Pick ONE. Never stack.

## Rules

- **Length:** 800-1,200 words
- **Voice:** your BEST voice — warmest, most specific, most opinionated
- **Updates:** refresh every 6 months (archive pivots; new "greatest hits"; stats get updated)
- **Link everywhere:** in your bio, in every automated welcome email, in your Twitter pinned, in your LinkedIn featured

## Writing process

This post is NOT drafted like a regular weekly issue. It deserves:
- 2-3 full drafts
- 1-week gap between drafts (read with fresh eyes)
- Testing with 3-5 trusted readers before publishing
- Read aloud before finalizing (rhythm matters more here than anywhere)

## Draft template

```
Subject line: <e.g. "Start here — the 3 things I send every week">

<Opening — 2-3 sentences establishing who/who/why>

<The Why — 200-300 words on why this exists>

Here's what you'll get as a subscriber:
- <outcome 1>
- <outcome 2>
- <outcome 3>
- <outcome 4>

How it works:
<Cadence + format + paid tier info + reply expectation>

If you're new, start with these 3 issues:
<links>

One ask: <single CTA>

— <your name>
```

## The 7-Section Deep Template (Dickie Bush)

A more detailed welcome post structure, focused on building trust quickly and signaling deep niche understanding.

### Section 1: Welcome
```
Welcome to [Newsletter Name]!

I'm [Name], and every [cadence] I send [brief value description].

My take: [One-sentence POV that sets you apart from the consensus].
```

### Section 2: Who Is This For?
Define 3 audience archetypes — readers should see themselves in at least one:
```
This newsletter is for you if:
- [Archetype 1: Specific job/role + situation]
- [Archetype 2: Specific goal or aspiration]
- [Archetype 3: Specific problem they're experiencing]
```

### Section 3: Biggest Problems
List 10 problems your niche faces. Showing you understand 10 problems signals deeper expertise than claiming to solve 1.
```
If you're reading this, you probably deal with:
1. [Problem]
2. [Problem]
...
10. [Problem]
```

### Section 4: Tangible, Repeatable Value
Don't describe your newsletter as "valuable content." Name the weekly asset:
```
Every [day], you'll get:
- [Specific asset 1 — template, prompt, analysis, framework]
- [Specific asset 2]
- [Specific asset 3]

This isn't a newsletter full of opinions. It's a [weekly tool/resource] for [outcome].
```

### Section 5: Who Are You?
Credentials relevant to why you're qualified for this specific niche:
```
I'm [Name]:
- [Most relevant role or company]
- [Supporting credential]
- [Key result that matters to this audience]
```

### Section 6: Popular Posts
Add after ~10 issues. Curate 3-5 best pieces:
```
If you're new, start here:
- [Title] — [One-line description of what they'll get]
- [Title] — [One-line description]
- [Title] — [One-line description]
```

### Section 7: CTA
One ask only. Pick based on your current goal:
```
Growth:     "Forward this to one person who'd find it valuable."
Revenue:    "Upgrade to [Paid Tier] — [brief benefit]. [Link]"
Engagement: "Reply with [specific question]. I read every one."
```

## 3 Advanced Welcome Post Upgrades (Dickie Bush)

Add these after your initial Welcome Post is live and tested:

### Upgrade 1: Welcome Video (30-60 seconds)
Embed a short video at the top of your Welcome Post. Script:
- Who you are (1 sentence)
- Who this is for (1 sentence)
- What they'll get (1-2 sentences)
- One CTA ("hit subscribe" / "reply to me")

**Why it works:** Video creates parasocial connection before the reader reads a single issue. Faces convert better than text alone.

### Upgrade 2: Subscriber Survey (Typeform)
Embed a 3-5 question survey immediately after the Welcome intro. Recommended questions:
1. What's your biggest challenge with [your niche]?
2. What type of content do you find most valuable?
3. What would make this newsletter a must-read for you?

**Why it works:** Responses inform your content calendar AND signal to the reader that you care about them specifically. Use Typeform (free tier works). Embed directly; don't link out.

### Upgrade 3: Paid Educational Email Course
Offer a free 5-7 email mini-course as a lead magnet linked from the Welcome Post. Each email delivers one lesson on a core topic in your niche.

**Why it works:** Multi-touch trust-building. Readers who complete the email course convert to paid at 2-3x the rate of those who only read the newsletter. Requires Substack drip (experimental) or Kit integration.

## About Page Template

**Rule: Do not write a separate About Page.** Copy your Welcome Post content verbatim.

**The only structural changes:**
1. Add a Subscribe button at the very top (above the fold)
2. Add a Subscribe button halfway through (after "What you'll get")
3. Add a Subscribe button at the bottom (after the CTA)

**Platform notes:**
- Substack: Write in the Substack About page editor; it does not override your pinned post
- Ghost/Beehiiv: Duplicate the Welcome Post and add Subscribe embeds at 3 points
