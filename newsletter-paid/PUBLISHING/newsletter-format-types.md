# Newsletter Format Types + AI Writing Workflow

> Four proven newsletter structures and the AI-assisted production workflow to fill them consistently.

**Source:** Ben Adkins — The $100K/Year AI-Powered Newsletter

---

## The 4 Newsletter Format Types

Pick ONE as your primary format. Master it before adding others.

---

### Format 1: The Curator

**Best for:** Information-dense niches where your audience needs a trusted filter.

**Structure:**
1. Subject line — "The [number] best [things] this week"
2. Opening — Brief personal context on why this selection matters
3. 5-7 curated items, each with:
   - Source/link
   - 2-3 sentence summary
   - Your original commentary ("why this matters")
4. Closing — The one thing you want them to take away

**Read time:** 5-10 minutes
**AI use:** Research and summarize sources. You add the editorial POV.

---

### Format 2: The Teacher

**Best for:** Skill-building niches where you have expertise to transfer.

**Structure:**
1. Subject line — Benefit + specificity ("How to [result] in [timeframe]")
2. Opening — State the one thing they'll know by the end
3. Main lesson in 3-5 sections (each: header + explanation + example)
4. Closing — The single action they should take today

**Read time:** 5-8 minutes
**AI use:** Generate first draft from bullet outline. You refine voice + add examples.

---

### Format 3: The Insider

**Best for:** Industry niches where access and timing create the value.

**Structure:**
1. Subject line — "What's actually happening with [topic]"
2. Opening — Set the scene (what changed, what most people are missing)
3. 2-3 insider takes with original analysis
4. Optional: interview excerpt or exclusive data point
5. Closing — What to watch next

**Read time:** 6-10 minutes
**AI use:** Research and compile public data. You provide interpretation and insider framing.

---

### Format 4: The Toolmaster

**Best for:** Tech/productivity niches with audiences that love discovering new tools.

**Structure:**
1. Subject line — "The [tool] I wish I'd found sooner"
2. Opening — Problem this tool solves
3. Tool overview: what it is, how it works, who it's for
4. Step-by-step use case or tutorial
5. Pricing/access info
6. Your verdict + CTA

**Read time:** 4-7 minutes
**AI use:** Draft the tutorial section. You test the tool and add real screenshots/results.

## The AI Writing Workflow (6 Steps)

Applies to all 4 formats. Total time: 45-90 minutes per issue.

**Step 1 — Research (15-20 min)**
- Use Perplexity or Claude to gather relevant sources
- Prompt: "Find the 5 most interesting developments in [niche] this week"
- Save 3-5 solid sources to a scratch doc

**Step 2 — Generate draft (10-15 min)**
```
Prompt template:
"Write a [FORMAT TYPE] newsletter for [AUDIENCE] on the topic of [TOPIC].

Use these sources and facts: [paste research]

Tone: [conversational/analytical/expert]
Length: [target word count]
Include: [specific sections you want]
Avoid: [topics, phrases, or angles to skip]"
```

**Step 3 — Edit for voice and accuracy (15-20 min)**
- Read the draft aloud
- Replace generic phrases with your actual voice
- Verify all facts and links
- Cut anything that doesn't add value

**Step 4 — Add personal touches (5-10 min)**
- Add one personal story or observation
- Reference something current in your work
- Include a genuine opinion (not a diplomatic non-take)

**Step 5 — Format for platform (10 min)**
- Break into short paragraphs
- Add headers for scannability
- Include subject line + preview text
- Test on mobile before sending

**Step 6 — Test send and review (5 min)**
- Send to yourself first
- Check links, formatting, images
- Read as a subscriber would

## Subject Line Formulas by Format

| Format | Best Subject Line Angle |
|--------|------------------------|
| Curator | "The [N] [things] worth your time this week" |
| Teacher | "How to [result] without [common obstacle]" |
| Insider | "What nobody is saying about [topic]" |
| Toolmaster | "The [tool] I wish I'd found sooner" |

**Preview text rule:** Never repeat the subject line. Add context or curiosity that builds on it.

```
Subject: How to double your open rates in 30 days
Preview: It's not your content. It's this one thing.
```

---

## Quality Checklist Before Sending

- [ ] Subject line creates genuine curiosity or promises clear value
- [ ] Opening hook is strong enough to keep reading
- [ ] Every section delivers on what was promised
- [ ] At least ONE thing the reader couldn't find by Googling
- [ ] Personal touch that no AI could have written
- [ ] Single clear CTA or takeaway at the end
- [ ] Preview text adds to subject line, doesn't repeat it
