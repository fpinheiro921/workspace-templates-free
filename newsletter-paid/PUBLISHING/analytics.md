## Quarterly review

Every 12 weeks, aggregate:
- Best-performing pillar (by opens / clicks / replies)
- Best-performing angle (from 10 Magical Ways)
- Best-performing hook type
- Best-performing template

Use findings to shape next quarter's `90-day-calendar.md`.

## The Golden Metric

**Revenue is the only metric that matters.**

Every other metric — open rate, landing page conversion, unsubscribe rate, subscriber count — is a leading indicator of revenue. That's all they are.

> *"Open Rate doesn't pay the rent."* — Dickie Bush

Most creators optimize vanity metrics instead of the one that matters. They track how many subscribers they have, how fast the list grew, how high their open rate is. None of that is the goal.

**Use all other metrics as diagnostic tools, not success measures:**

| Metric | What it diagnoses |
|--------|------------------|
| Open rate | Subject line quality + sender reputation |
| Click rate | Body content relevance + CTA clarity |
| Reply rate | Engagement depth + trust level |
| Unsubscribe rate | Audience fit + content relevance |
| Landing page conversion | Positioning clarity + offer strength |

Low on any of these → fix the underlying issue → check if revenue responds. That's the only confirmation loop that matters.

## What NOT to track

- Day-by-day fluctuations (don't read noise as signal)
- Vanity metrics without a decision attached (what will you DO differently based on this number?)
- Metrics you can't influence (individual reader behavior)
