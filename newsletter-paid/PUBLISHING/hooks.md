# Hooks — Opening Moves

The first 1-2 sentences of every newsletter. If these don't land, nothing else matters.

Full framework in `_PATTERNS/hook-library-core.md` part C (6 hook types).

## The 6 hook types

1. **Strong declarative statement** — `Your niche isn't your ceiling. It's your entry point.`
2. **Thought-provoking question** — `What if niching into "boring" industries is the shortcut?`
3. **Controversial opinion** — `Staying a generalist "to keep options open" is how writers stay broke for 5 years.`
4. **Vulnerable statement** — `I spent 3 years calling myself "versatile." I was afraid to commit.`
5. **Weird / unique insight or stat** — `1,500 ghostwriters in PGA. Zero started with an audience.`
6. **Meaningful moment-in-time** — `In 2019, I was $40k in debt. Today I run a 7-figure agency.`

## Rules

- **Cliffhanger-ending** — the reader should want the NEXT sentence
- **Tangible language** — "stop dreading Monday" not "be happier"
- **Specificity** — names, numbers, situations (not "some people")
- **No clichés** — "In today's fast-paced world", "Let's be real", "Here's the thing" — all banned
- **No emojis in the hook** (safe for body if voice permits)

## Hook rotation strategy

Across a quarter, rotate hook types so you don't always open with the same move. Suggested rotation for 12-week calendar:
- 4× strong declarative
- 2× question
- 2× controversial
- 2× vulnerable
- 1× unique insight
- 1× moment-in-time

This feels diverse to the reader without feeling random.

## Hook-to-template pairing

Certain hooks work better with certain templates:

| Template | Best-fit hook types |
|---|---|
| Tactical How-To | Strong declarative, Unique insight |
| Contrarian Take | Controversial opinion, Strong declarative |
| Personal Essay | Moment-in-time, Vulnerable |
| Curated Insight | Unique insight, Question |
| Framework Breakdown | Question, Strong declarative |
